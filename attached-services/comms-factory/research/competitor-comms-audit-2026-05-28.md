# Competitor Comms Audit — 2026-05-28

Audit of how named crypto/web3 orgs handle feature/launch comms. Inputs for comms-factory's launch-announcement pipeline (Hyperliquid Spark, Synthetics Perps, Bridge/On-Off-ramps).

Web research only. Recent ~6 months focus. Where useful, posts classified in canonical Mirodan/Laban vocabulary against the locked Infinex tempi (Commanding · Practical · Sombre · Irradiant · Sociable).

---

## TL;DR — Five headlines that change Infinex's three launches

1. **Hyperliquid's "the fact, alone" voice is the structural baseline.** "Permissionless spot deploys are now live on mainnet." "$HYPE, the native token of the Hyperliquid L1, is live." No adjectives, no hashtags, no exclamation marks. This is Commanding-led with a Practical body — and it travels better than any of the marketing-y competitors. Infinex's Spark launch should sit in this register, not the Phantom one. Phantom-tier motion treatment ≠ Phantom-tier copy.
2. **Phantom's playbook is "Today, we're rolling out X to everyone on Phantom" + chain emoji + bullet list of three concrete user verbs.** Lock that shape for any Infinex integration-class announcement (Spark, Synthetics, Bridge). It's Sociable-led, Practical-bodied, ends Irradiant. Three bullets max. Verbs first.
3. **Kalshi's "Timeless" tease pattern is the highest-leverage move Infinex hasn't done yet.** Cryptic torus video → codename → city + date → product reveal a week later. For the Hyperliquid Spark launch — Phantom-tier motion — the right play is NOT a one-shot launch tweet, it's a 5-7 day tease arc anchored by one signature object (Kalshi: torus; Polymarket: data card; Pendle: split). Spend the motion budget on the object, not the launch day.
4. **Pendle's "problem-first, not novelty-first" body works for hard products.** Boros didn't open with "introducing"; it opened with "Perp exchanges facilitate $150-$200B of trading volume daily" and named the gap. For Synthetics Perps — which is genuinely a hard product to pitch in 280 chars — the Pendle frame beats the Hyperliquid terse-fact frame. Practical-led, Commanding-bodied, Sombre prep.
5. **Avoid the four most common slop patterns:** (a) "Innovative/revolutionary/game-changing" — caught by validator already, but worth flagging that 6+ orgs in this audit hit it. (b) Token-launch tweets disguised as product launches (Rainbow, Sport.Fun) — these collapse "we shipped a thing" into "we shipped a token" and look mercenary. (c) Hashtag stacking (`#BuildOnBase`, `#HyperLiquid`) — Hyperliquid itself doesn't use them; neither should Infinex. (d) Emoji-as-bullet replacement when bullets would do (Phantom's 💧 droplets on the Sui launch read cheaper than the same post with `→` arrows).

## Tier 1 — deep dive

### Hyperliquid

**Channels:** X (@HyperliquidX), in-app announcements panel, Telegram (@hyperliquid_announcements), docs.

**Recent examples (verbatim or near-verbatim from search):**

1. *"Permissionless spot deploys are now live on mainnet. Deployers are strongly recommended to try testnet first to avoid issues on mainnet. No user-deployed spot assets are officially endorsed. Do not trade assets you are unfamiliar with and do not understand the risks for. DYOR."* ([X](https://x.com/HyperliquidX/status/1793967681986806153))
2. *"ETH deposits, withdrawals, and spot trading are now live on Hyperliquid. Deposit ETH on [link]. Trade spot ETH at [link]."* ([X](https://x.com/HyperliquidX/status/1905293958835818573))
3. *"$HYPE, the native token of the Hyperliquid L1, is live."* ([X](https://x.com/HyperliquidX/status/1862402701705060486))

**Copy patterns:**
- Fact-density: extreme. Subject + "is now live on [where]" is the dominant shape.
- No openers like "Excited to announce" or "Today we're launching". Often no first-person "we" at all — the *thing* is the subject, not the team.
- Risk language is included inline ("DYOR", "do not trade assets you are unfamiliar with"). Reads as legal-flavored but functions as voice: anti-marketing posture.
- No hashtags. No emoji. No exclamation marks.
- Long-tail content (HIP-3 explainers, validator changes) lives in the in-app announcements panel and links out for detail. Twitter is the headline only.

**Visual treatment:** Mostly text-only tweets. When images appear they're small data screenshots or terminal-style charts. Founder Jeff occasionally posts BTC perpetual liquidity screenshots comparing Hyperliquid vs Binance — the screenshot IS the visual; no template chrome around it. Anti-design as a brand statement.

**Cadence:** Sporadic but high-signal. Feature launches when ready, not on a schedule. HIP-4 (May 11, 2026) and validator expansion (May 18, 2026) are recent examples — both got single tweets, no thread.

**Mirodan/Laban classification:** Commanding-led (Punching release, all the gravity in the verb "live"). Practical-bodied where structure exists. Sombre-prep is implicit — the absence of celebration IS the gravity. Anti-Irradiant by construction. This is the voice closest to Infinex's "banker-turned-trailblazer" image when stripped of warmth.

### Polymarket

**Channels:** X (@Polymarket), blog, in-product "X Cards" integration (X named Polymarket its official prediction-market partner June 2025), iOS app.

**Recent examples (paraphrased; few exact-copy hits via search):**

1. US re-launch announcement (May 2026) — framed around "back legally in the US" with sports + elections as the wedges, challenging Kalshi as named competitor.
2. Tweet Markets product — uses the prediction-market card itself as the post (e.g., "Elon Musk # tweets May 12-19, 2026" rendered as data card embedded in feed).
3. Perpetual futures product (race with Kalshi, late April 2026) — announcement positioned as response/parallel to Kalshi's tease.

**Copy patterns:**
- Polymarket's most-effective comms object is **the market itself, not the tweet.** The X Cards integration means a market embed often does the work of a launch post. The headline question + odds + chart = native ad.
- When they do write launch copy, register is news-anchor neutral: "Polymarket is back in the US with sports and elections markets." No hype.
- They lean on third-party-as-author: prediction-market odds *are* the news, journalists embed them, the brand benefits without writing copy.

**Visual treatment:** This is the single most-studied template family in crypto comms. Three view modes (per the PolyFakeIt/Fake-A-Polymarket clones, which copied them): Chart view, Card view, Bet view. The "Card view" — outcome + probability + volume — is what mockup tools replicate because it's instantly recognizable. Polymarket's brand uses Inter as primary typeface; #2E5CFF is the load-bearing blue (and listed as a claimed-palette in comms-factory's validator).

**Cadence:** Constant — every notable market launches as its own micro-comms. Major product launches (US relaunch, perps) get standalone tweets. Markets themselves get auto-generated cards.

**Mirodan/Laban classification:** Practical-led on the product (data card = "explain the structure, the structure justifies itself"), Sociable on the X embed flow (the cards are inherently shareable / inherently dialog-with-audience). Notably NOT Commanding — Polymarket lets the numbers carry the weight, doesn't punch.

### Phantom

**Channels:** X (@phantom), blog (phantom.com/learn/blog), in-app messaging (Phantom Chat launching 2026), mobile push.

**Recent examples (verbatim from X):**

1. *"Today, we're rolling out @Base to everyone on Phantom 🔵 You can now: → Swap tokens between Base and Solana → Access your favorite DeFi and NFT apps → Buy ETH and USDC on Base right inside your wallet"* ([X](https://x.com/phantom/status/1861108202022318470))
2. *"Today, we're rolling out Sui to everyone on Phantom 🌊 You can now: 💧 Swap tokens between Sui, Ethereum, Base, and Solana 💧 Access your favorite @SuiNetwork apps 💧 Buy SUI from right inside your wallet"* ([X](https://x.com/phantom/status/1921976542931960299))
3. Base launch blog post — opens *"It's time to bring the next billion users on-chain…"*, runs ~500-600 words, signature phrases: "One wallet for everything", "Safe and secure", "It's 100% opt-in", closes with `#BuildOnBase`. ([blog](https://phantom.com/learn/blog/base-on-phantom))

**Copy patterns:**
- The signature post shape: **"Today, we're rolling out [thing] to everyone on Phantom [chain emoji]"** + bullet list of three concrete user verbs ("Swap...", "Access...", "Buy...").
- Three bullets is the lock. Never four. The verbs are user-action verbs, not feature names.
- Chain emoji as identifier (🔵 Base, 🌊 Sui) — branding-by-color via emoji.
- Blog posts run hype-but-accessible. "Intrigued?" as a register tell. Hashtags do appear (`#BuildOnBase`).
- "100% opt-in" is a recurring trust phrase — Phantom positions every new feature as user-controlled.

**Visual treatment:** This is the **high-end motion** reference operator flagged. Phantom commissioned motion design by Bakken & Bæck for the brand identity refresh; launch tweets often include a square-aspect motion clip with the wallet UI demonstrating the feature, gradient backgrounds, soft physics on UI elements. Phantom does NOT use static screenshots for launches — every launch ships with motion.

**Cadence:** Chain integrations roughly quarterly (Base ~Nov 2024, Sui ~May 2025, others). Smaller features get blog posts only. Major launches get tweet + blog + motion clip + sometimes a co-tweet from the partner chain.

**Mirodan/Laban classification:** Sociable-led ("we're rolling out...to everyone on Phantom" — direct address, inclusive), Practical-bodied (three user-verb bullets = explain the structure), Irradiant close (chain emoji + warm "right inside your wallet"). The motion treatment carries Flow-stress. Notably the COPY is warmer than Infinex's Stable+Penetrating placement; the **motion** is what Infinex should steal, not the voice.

### Kalshi

**Channels:** X (@Kalshi), LinkedIn (CEO Tarek Mansour posted the "Timeless" tease on LinkedIn first, not X), blog.

**Recent examples:**

1. **"Timeless" tease (April 13, 2026):** CEO Tarek Mansour posts a cryptic video on LinkedIn — dark glassy green torus rotating, "Timeless" fades in, then date `4.27.2026` and location `NY, NYC`. No product info. No copy in the body. ([context](https://x.com/cryptounfolded/status/2046628319207059613))
2. **Launch (April 27, 2026):** Crypto perpetual futures go live, framed as "no expiration date" event contracts. Polymarket announces parallel offering within hours.
3. **Consumer-protection hub + IC360 SelfExclude partnership (May 2026)** — corporate-PR register, distinct from product-launch register.

**Copy patterns:**
- Tease-then-reveal is the load-bearing pattern. The product is named (Timeless) before the product is described.
- CEO-led announcements via LinkedIn first, then X. This is a counterintuitive move — using a "boring" platform (LinkedIn) for the highest-stakes tease BECAUSE it confused crypto-Twitter and the confusion drove the discussion.
- Body copy at launch is regulatory-flavored ("CFTC-regulated", "perpetual event contracts") — Kalshi leans into the "we have the permission slip" register that distinguishes them from offshore competitors.

**Visual treatment:** The torus. One signature 3D object — dark glassy green — rotating in loop. **This is the lesson:** spend the motion budget on ONE object that BECOMES the launch's identity, not on demonstrating a UI. Pendle does the same with the principal-token/yield-token split. Polymarket does the same with the prediction card.

**Cadence:** Major launches teased ~1-2 weeks ahead. Routine markets don't get launch comms; they appear in-product.

**Mirodan/Laban classification:** The torus tease is pure Sombre — Pressing prep, withholding, weight. The launch tweet itself shifts to Commanding (the fact, lands). Reveal arc is Sombre→Commanding, which is precisely the multi-beat arc Infinex's voice spec describes for `data-card-official` (Sombre opens, Commanding lands).

### Pendle

**Channels:** X (@pendle_fi), Medium blog (boros-fi/pendle), docs, in-app, governance forum.

**Recent examples:**

1. **Boros launch post (Aug 2025, Medium):** Opens *"Pendle remains steadfast in our mission of becoming the premier yield-trading platform. This ambition extends to every yield in the world."* Pivots to *"Perp exchanges facilitate $150-$200B of trading volume daily"* — market-scale framing. ([Medium](https://medium.com/boros-fi/boros-by-pendle-yield-trading-with-margin-63d026dc7399))
2. **Boros $10B notional milestone (early 2026):** ~2.7× faster than Pendle V2's earlier record. Posted as "Boros hits $10B notional" with chart attached.
3. **March 2026 roadmap updates:** "cross-platform funding-rate arbitrage made super easy" — operator-direct language, not marketing.

**Copy patterns:**
- **Problem-first, not novelty-first.** Pendle pitches hard products (yield tokenization, funding rate trading) by naming the market opportunity in $-volume terms first, then naming the gap, then introducing the product as the obvious response.
- Mission re-anchoring at the top of major posts ("Pendle remains steadfast in our mission of..."). Acts as a Sombre prep — weight before the reveal.
- Uses concrete users as proof points (Ethena's protocol sustainability problem cited as the Boros use case).
- Token-economic detail surfaced openly. Not hidden behind links.

**Visual treatment:** Pendle's brand has a signature **green/teal split** (PT vs YT — principal token vs yield token rendered as two-color semantic split). This is the `split` template family in comms-factory's card schema, and Pendle is the canonical reference. The Boros sub-brand uses a green palette adjacent to Pendle's main #1BE3C2 (claimed-palette in validator).

**Cadence:** Major launches roughly quarterly. Boros milestones (TVL, OI, expiry expansions) posted weekly. Educational threads roughly monthly.

**Mirodan/Laban classification:** Practical-led (the structure is the pitch), Sombre prep (mission re-anchor), Commanding land (the new product is named flat). Notably Pendle does NOT do Irradiant lifts — there's no "the future is here" closing. The closing is the data.

## Patterns Infinex should STEAL

Numbered by priority + tagged with which of the three launches each serves.

### 1. Hyperliquid's terse-fact tweet shape — for ALL THREE launches as the launch-day default
*"$X is now live on $Y"* with no adjectives, no hashtags, no exclamation marks. Risk language inline. The thing is the subject, not the team.

- **Hyperliquid Spark:** *"Hyperliquid is now live on Infinex."* (or inverse, depending on framing). Single sentence. Add motion clip. Done.
- **Synthetics Perps:** *"Synthetics perps are now live on Infinex Perps."* + one-line about what synthetics means.
- **Bridge / on-off-ramps:** *"On-ramps and off-ramps are live in Infinex Bridge."* Plus one line about coverage.

**Validator implication:** Add a positive-pattern check (or just a memory) that flags any launch tweet over 200 characters as candidate for trim. Hyperliquid launches average ~140.

### 2. Kalshi's tease-then-reveal arc — for Hyperliquid Spark specifically
This is the Phantom-tier-motion play. Operator wants the launch to feel premium; the way to spend the motion budget is on ONE signature object that becomes Spark's identity, teased for 5-7 days, then revealed.

- Day -7: motion clip of the object alone, no text body, no link. Object should be Infinex-Hyperliquid color-fused (purple/blue?) and abstract enough to be ownable but concrete enough to be screenshot-able.
- Day -3: object + word ("Spark"). No date.
- Day -1: object + date. No description.
- Day 0: Hyperliquid-style terse-fact tweet + the motion clip (now contextualized).

**Why:** spending the motion budget on a UI demo is a one-time impression. Spending it on an object that recurs across the launch arc is a brand-building moment. Kalshi's torus is the proof.

### 3. Pendle's problem-first body — for Synthetics Perps
Synthetics is the hardest of the three to pitch in 280 chars because the value isn't obvious. Pendle's Boros opener — *"Perp exchanges facilitate $150-$200B daily. Funding rates lack reliable hedging."* — gives a model: name market size + name gap + introduce product.

- Synthetics Perps draft: *"Perp markets price crypto. Now they price the world. Synthetic perps on Infinex — stocks, FX, commodities, the long tail."* (Sketch only — voice owner will workshop.)
- This is Practical-led (explain the structure), Sombre-prep (the absence is the weight), Commanding land (the product names itself).

### 4. Phantom's three-bullet user-verb shape — for the Bridge launch and any integration-class follow-ups
*"Today, X is live on Infinex. You can now: → [verb] → [verb] → [verb]."* Three bullets. Verbs first. No hashtags.

- **Bridge launch fits this perfectly.** Bridge IS a user-verb product (deposit, send, swap, withdraw across chains). The bullets write themselves.
- **Spark could use this as the body** under a Hyperliquid-style headline.
- **Synthetics less so** — verbs are too abstract for the bullets to land.

**Note:** Use `→` not emoji droplets. Phantom's 🌊 droplets on the Sui launch read cheaper than the arrow form on the Base launch.

### 5. Polymarket's "the artifact IS the comms" — for all three, ongoing
The data-card-official template family in comms-factory is built for this. Each launch should ship with a native shareable card that can stand alone in feed without the tweet body. This is the X Cards integration mental model.

- **Spark:** a data card showing Hyperliquid integration metrics (volumes, supported markets).
- **Synthetics Perps:** a data card showing the synthetic universe at launch (assets, leverage caps).
- **Bridge:** a data card showing supported chains + rails (a split-template fits — semantic chain pairs).

### 6. Stargate's 4-word slogan — for the Bridge launch
"Bring the Bridge Home" is the load-bearing brand asset of the entire Stargate→LayerZero merger comms. A short, ownable, emotional phrase that anchors a launch outperforms a paragraph of body copy.

- Bridge launch needs one. Suggested shape, NOT verbatim: 3-5 words, includes a verb or a destination noun.
- This belongs in brand-factory's voice spec work, not in comms-factory directly. Flagging because the SHAPE is the steal-able lesson.

### 7. Pear Protocol's educational track — alongside Synthetics Perps
Synthetics is hard to pitch in 280 chars. Pair the launch tweet with a parallel "how synthetic perps work" thread or micro-course. Pear did this with their pair-trading course — same week as the Hyperliquid integration. Two-track comms (launch + education) doubles the surface area without doubling the work, because the educational content slots into the same launch arc.

### 8. Wildcat's hero-copy posture — for any Infinex product page that ships alongside
*"On-Chain Credit without Excessive Collateral"* is a perfect hero. Subject + the differentiating clause. No verbs. No marketing. Anti-design as differentiation.

- Synthetics Perps hero candidate: *"Perpetuals beyond crypto."* Or: *"Global markets, on-chain."*
- This is brand-factory territory but the lesson ports: Infinex's hero copy should NOT be a sentence with a verb — it should be a noun-phrase that names the differentiation.

## Patterns Infinex should AVOID

### 1. "Excited to announce / Thrilled to / Stay tuned" — already in validator's `rejectAIslop`
6+ orgs in this audit hit some variant. Sport.Fun's "groundbreaking" framing, Rainbow's "That feeling when..." enthusiasm pump, Phantom's "It's time to bring the next billion users on-chain" — all flag-able. Hyperliquid is the only Tier-1 org that consistently avoids this register.

### 2. Hashtag stacking
`#BuildOnBase`, `#HyperLiquid`, etc. Phantom does it; Hyperliquid doesn't. The orgs whose voice Infinex's spec is closest to (Hyperliquid, Wildcat, Pendle) all avoid hashtags entirely. Recommend zero-tolerance — promote to validator rule as `rejectHashtags` (separate from `rejectListicleVoice`).

### 3. Token-launch comms absorbing product comms
Rainbow Wallet's Q1 2026 was almost entirely RNBW-token comms even though they shipped real product features. Sport.Fun had the same pattern around their TGE. Stargate's identity got absorbed into ZRO. **Infinex has no token comms problem because Infinex has no token launch in scope** — but if/when a points/token program ships, do NOT let the financial-instrument comms eat the product comms. Keep them on separate weeks.

### 4. Emoji-as-bullet-replacement when arrows would do
Phantom's 💧 droplets on the Sui launch read cheaper than `→` arrows on the Base launch. The arrow form is universal-shape; the droplet form is theme-chained-to-Sui and won't travel to Infinex.

### 5. Adjective-stacking on a single noun
Trust Wallet's "free, open-source wallet connection library for web3 developers that offers native EVM, Bitcoin, and Solana support, full UI customization, and Apache 2.0 licensing" — five adjectival modifiers before the noun. Validator catches some via `rejectAIslop`; the broader pattern is "more than 2 modifiers on the launch-noun is slop". Worth adding to allergen catalog.

### 6. Multiplicative claims without basis ("10× faster", "next billion users")
TON does the "10× faster" thing and it works because they shipped Catchain 2.0 receipts. Phantom does "next billion users" with no basis and it reads as filler. Rule of thumb: any multiplicative or scale claim must be tied to deployed_facts in the card. If it isn't, drop it. (This is implicit in the existing fact-grounding pipeline; flagging as an audit-surface failure mode.)

### 7. Founder-as-spokesperson register when the team isn't a founder cult
TON (Durov), Wildcat (Laurence Day), Pear (Huf quoted in PR) all use founder voice productively. Infinex doesn't have a single founder face in the same way — the operator/team is collective. Borrowing founder-voice patterns without a founder-face will read inauthentic. Stay in team-voice register.

### 8. PR-distribution leakage into voice
NEAR Intents uses PRNewswire for big launches and the PR-register language ("agentic economy", "chain abstraction", "unified liquidity") leaks into their X posts. The Egocentric tempo failure mode. Validator's `rejectAIslop` catches "innovative/cutting-edge"; worth extending to "agentic economy" / "chain abstraction" / abstract-noun-stack-of-3+. The drift here is from Practical into Egocentric — speaking in tongues only the team understands.

### 9. "Endgame" and other finality claims
Ekubo's "AMM Endgame" is bold but only works because StarkNet shipped concentrated-liquidity that genuinely was a structural improvement. Most "endgame" claims age badly. Avoid unless there's a structural argument we can defend — and even then, defer to brand-factory's tonal call.

### 10. Heavy first-person ("we") in the headline
Hyperliquid almost never uses "we" in the headline. Phantom always does. Both work, but Infinex's Stable+Penetrating placement is closer to Hyperliquid's posture. Default to subject-first ("X is live on Infinex") rather than team-first ("We're launching X"). The "we" can come in the body if needed.
