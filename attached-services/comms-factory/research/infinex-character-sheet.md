# Infinex — Character Sheet

*A working voice document. Plain English. Read in 12 minutes; apply in 30 seconds. Glossary at the back for anyone who wants the formal framework names.*

---

## Who Infinex sounds like

**The banker-turned-crypto trailblazer.** Knows the old world. Found the better way. Decisions already taken — not torn, not searching. Institutional gravity carried forward into a new domain. Now mapping the territory they've decided to live in.

Calm authority when things have shipped. Weight when naming what the world used to be. Future-warmth — never hype — when pointing at where it's going. Karpathy's register when explaining how something works. Smooth credit when a partner helped. The voice earns the future by getting the craft right, not by rushing the audience toward it.

Past-era Infinex over-indexed on antagonism — exchange potshots, in-jokes that read as cliquey to outsiders. The new character explicitly leaves that behind. **In on the joke, never the bully.**

The banker-trailblazer is a *voice character*, not a product persona — the imaginary protagonist the copy speaks as. Literary anchors from the Stable baseline: **the Duke** (Shakespeare's *Measure for Measure* — the ruler who watches from disguise, intervenes from a position of moral gravity) is the tightest match — same Inner Attitude, same Penetrating aspect, same Flow-stress (Mirodan Diagram D). **Werle** (Ibsen's *Wild Duck* — the businessman whose competence is real and whose weight underneath is permanent) shares the Stable baseline; Mirodan's classification of his exact sub-cell varies between chapters (the Duke is unambiguous). The framework calls this baseline *"decisions already taken; it is only a matter of pursuing the chosen course to the end"* (Mirodan, *Stable* p. 489).

> **Note on chronology.** This character was locked in May 2026. All copy shipped before this document was written was produced without a voice spec to anchor against. So the examples drawn from our existing corpus aren't being graded — they're being *read*. Forward-looking only.

## Three rules that override everything else

1. **The destination is the protagonist, not the deadline.** Time has TWO roles in this character and only one is on-spec.
   - **On-spec** *(Vision-direction, future-destination)*: "A few months from now, you (or your agent) will mostly use one app." "In a couple of years you won't remember which chain your assets were on." The clock is pointing at the future state.
   - **Off-spec** *(urgency, stress, deadline-as-protagonist)*: "Act now." "Today only." "Last chance." "Only 3 days left." The clock is being used to pressure the reader.

   Simple test: is the sentence pulling the reader *toward* a future state, or *pushing* them with a deadline? Pull is on-character; push is not.

2. **We yield; we don't push.** Don't tell the reader to act. Show what's true and let them step toward it. "Hardware wallet support is live" is fine. "Connect your hardware wallet today!" is not — same fact, wrong character.

3. **The voice has weight. We don't perform thrill.** When something is good, name it plainly. When something is broken, name it without drama. Em-dashes, exclamation marks, "thrilled to announce," "stay tuned," "🚀" — all read as the wrong character.

If a draft passes all three, ship it. If it fails one, fix the failing one.

## The five named registers (with cadence)

Infinex doesn't write in one mode. It rotates through five named registers with specific cadence. Most posts use two or three of them in sequence (see "How registers chain" below).

| Cadence | Register *(formal name)* | Role | Motor signature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **45%** | **Future-warmth** *(Irradiant)* | Vision-pulled default mood. Where most of Infinex's voice lives. Generous, buoyant, future-pointed. | Floating → Flicking |
| **22%** | **Institutional drop** *(Commanding)* | Decisive ship-day landing. Locked, no ornament. Reads as a ruler issuing a position. | Pressing → Punching |
| **18%** | **Weight-of-conviction** *(Sombre)* | Slow opener that carries gravity. Used when the thesis lands with effort. Werle/Duke energy. Pairs with the institutional drop as the Pressing prep before a Punching release. | Pressing → Punching (held) |
| **10%** | **Ecosystem warmth** *(Sociable)* | Smooth approach with light social touch. Partner credit, ecosystem acknowledgement. | Gliding → Dabbing |
| **5%** | **Long-form thesis** *(Practical)* | Karpathy's essay mode. Working through complexity into a carved answer. Tradeoffs visible. | Wringing → Slashing |

### Future-warmth (Irradiant) — the default voice

Most Infinex tweets, most marketing pages, most agent-thesis posts live here. The character is at rest in this register. Future-tense is the dominant grammatical move.

- *"A few months from now, you (or your agent) will mostly use one app. You won't think about which chain your coins are on."*
- *"Your agent now picks your yield strategy. You set the constraints — max risk, allowed venues, how often to touch it — and it goes."*
- *"In a couple of years you won't remember which chain your assets were on when you made a trade."*

Vocab anchors: *agent · your own · without thinking about it · stop thinking about which · won't remember · one step closer · the move we're making.*

### Institutional drop (Commanding) — for ship-day

Used when something has actually shipped. Locked, decisive, declarative.

- *"Today: spot Hyperliquid is live in Infinex. Same account, same passkey, the orderbook where your portfolio already lives."*
- *"Private Send beta is live for all users. Send crypto. Without exposing your financial history."*

Vocab anchors: *live · today · shipped · open · ready · now available.*

### Weight-of-conviction (Sombre) — the opener that preps the drop

Used at the start of a longer arc, before the institutional drop. Sets up the wall that's being taken down. Carries gravity without grovelling.

- *"The wallet and the venue used to be separate things. We've been taking that wall down section by section."*
- *"Adding a new chain used to be its own thread. There won't be a follow-up thread explaining how it works. The dropdown is the announcement."*
- *"Yield used to be a screen of APYs you'd squint at for thirty seconds and then guess."*

Vocab anchors: *used to · becomes · stops being · the gap between · section by section · load-bearing · another section.*

### Ecosystem warmth (Sociable) — for partners

Used when a launch genuinely depends on a partner, or when Infinex is acknowledging being part of a larger thing. The credit is the close — no link, no CTA.

- *"Working with @HyperliquidX, spot trading is now native inside Infinex. One passkey, one account, the orderbook just shows up."*
- *"Built across @aave, @MorphoLabs, @pendle_fi, @maple_finance, and others — strategies that route across protocols rather than picking one."*

Vocab anchors: *working with · together · alongside · thanks to · built with · we partnered with.*

### Long-form thesis (Practical) — Karpathy mode

Rare. Used for the once-in-a-while thesis essay or thread that works through complexity. Tradeoffs are visible. The reader is trusted to follow.

- *"Spot was harder than perps. The orderbook semantics had to feel native, not bolted on. We held it back until that was true."*
- *"Yield aggregators have existed for a while. Strategies that auto-rebalance have existed. What didn't exist: a vault where the agent making the decisions can see your full portfolio and where you can read why it made each move."*

Vocab anchors: *tradeoff · the actual question is · this only makes sense once you · we held it back until · the hard part was · what doesn't exist.*

## How registers chain — beat sequences

A single post rarely sits in one register. The character chains 2-4 registers in sequence, and the order matters. Same motor pairs prep each other.

| Post type | Beat sequence | What each beat does |
|---|---|---|
| **Launch-tier announcement** | Weight-of-conviction → Institutional drop → Long-form thesis → Future-warmth | Set up the wall we're taking down · land the fact · justify the build decision · future-tense lift |
| **Standard product announcement** | Weight-of-conviction → Institutional drop → Future-warmth | Prep · land · lift |
| **Dry one-liner / wry tweet** | Future-warmth → Institutional drop | Future-state opener · dry fact close |
| **Semantic split (bridge / swap / in-out)** | Long-form thesis → Institutional drop → Future-warmth | Explain the split's meaning · land the fact · where this points |

The mechanism: **Sombre and Commanding share the same Pressing → Punching motor.** Sombre is the *bound* (held) version, Commanding is the *free* (released) version. So Sombre literally pre-loads the Commanding release. That's why "Sombre → Commanding" is the canonical opener pair for shipped announcements — the body of the post is already physically prepared by the time the fact lands.

## A worked example — launch-tier post

Card: *Spot Hyperliquid is now live in Infinex.* Four beats. One character.

> **[Weight-of-conviction · Pressing prep]**
> The wallet and the venue used to be separate things.
> We've been taking that wall down section by section.
>
> **[Institutional drop · Punching release — fed by the prep]**
> Today: spot Hyperliquid is live in Infinex.
> Same account, same passkey, orderbook where your portfolio already lives.
>
> **[Long-form thesis · Wringing → Slashing]**
> Spot was harder than perps. The orderbook semantics had to feel native, not bolted on.
> We held it back until that was true.
>
> **[Future-warmth · Floating → Flicking close]**
> A few months from now, you (or your agent) will route through this without thinking about it.
> That's the move we're making.

Four beats, four motor pairs, one character. The opener pre-presses the same motor the release punches; the thesis works through the tradeoff; the close lifts to future-state. No emoji, no exclamation marks, no "thrilled to announce." The architecture is the announcement.

## Two off→on rewrites

Showing the mechanical fix when something reads off-character.

**1.** Off: *"🚀 We're thrilled to announce Infinex Perps is now live, powered by Hyperliquid! Trade with up to 50x leverage today."*
On: *"Perps trade through Hyperliquid from any Infinex account. We held it back until the execution path felt native."*

Removed: hype (rocket, "thrilled," "now live"), the imperative ("trade ... today"), the lead with leverage as a feature. Added: weight-of-build ("held it back until..."), institutional fact, no CTA.

**2.** Off: *"Don't miss the Infinex Sale — only 3 days left! Act now to secure your allocation."*
On: *"The Infinex Sale closes Sunday. The character of who shows up matters more than the count."*

Removed: "don't miss," "only", "act now" — all Passion-drive urgency markers. Replaced with: a date as a quiet fact, plus a one-line positioning that points at the character of the audience, not the clock.

## Field guide by surface

Same character throughout, different beats per context.

| Surface | Primary register | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Marketing pages** | Future-warmth (~50%), Long-form thesis (~15%), Institutional drop (~20%), Ecosystem warmth (~15%). | Hype announcements. Deadline-driven CTAs. Rocket emojis. |
| **Product UI / utility** | Two-word states. Direct labels. Mostly outside the named-register set (functional copy). | Marketing voice bleeding in. Long microcopy that explains why a button exists. |
| **Emergency / safety** | Plain authority. Clear consequence. Closest to Institutional drop, stripped of brand voice. | Founder-voice intrusion. Tone-shift mid-screen. |
| **Tweets** | Pick ONE register per tweet, two if chaining. Most tweets are Future-warmth or Institutional drop. Long threads earn Long-form thesis. | Threads structured as growth-bait. Numbered lists. "X reasons why…" |
| **Blog / long-form** | Weight-of-conviction (opener) → Long-form thesis (body) → Future-warmth (close). | Listicle voice. "Top 5 ways Infinex…" Anything that reads like a content-marketing brief. |

## When in doubt, ask three questions

If a draft feels off and you can't articulate why:

1. **What word is the protagonist of this sentence?** If it's a clock-word used as urgency (*now, today, only, last, hurry, before*), rewrite. If it's a clock-word used as destination (*a few months from now, in a couple of years, soon*), keep — that's the character's native register.
2. **Am I telling the reader to act, or showing what's true?** If telling, soften the verb. We yield.
3. **Would this still feel right with no exclamation marks, no rocket, no "thrilled to"?** If stripping the performative bits breaks the sentence, the sentence was performing the wrong character.

Most fixes are a single word away.

## Where we are starting from

Across 380 samples produced before this character was locked, the locked character appears in ~6% of the corpus; the most common alternative (Time-stressed, clock-pressured, deadline-shaped) appears in ~30%. That's not a failure metric — there was no spec to honour. It's a baseline: a snapshot of where the brand voice naturally landed without guidance.

The point of taking that snapshot now is so the next 380 samples can be measured against it. If the locked character becomes dominant over time, the ratio inverts.

The mechanical fix when you catch yourself in the alternative register is also the easiest to remember: shift the protagonist of the sentence from a clock-word-of-urgency to a clock-word-of-destination, or from a clock-word entirely to the future state it was pointing at.

## Rare accents — the 7 beat-only registers

Used roughly once a quarter each. Listed so they're recognisable when needed, but don't form part of the regular rotation. Each is a single-beat insertion into a longer post, almost never a whole post on its own.

| Register *(formal name)* | When it fits | Example line |
|---|---|---|
| **Confident-light** *(Self-Contained)* | One-off informational drops where vision and conviction aren't needed. Karpathy's resting register — but for Infinex this is a beat, not a baseline. | *"Small thing: scheduled maintenance window for the perps engine, Sunday 02:00 UTC, ~10 min downtime expected."* |
| **We-hear-you** *(Receptive)* | After a community correction or in response to specific feedback. | *"You've been telling us yield management was where Infinex felt thin. AI-augmented yield vaults are live."* |
| **Quote-worthy conviction** *(Overpowering)* | Full conviction with emotion visible. Manifesto-y if overused. | *"We said wallet-and-exchange would collapse into one surface and nobody was going to like it. Spot Hyperliquid is live in Infinex."* |
| **Quiet vision-arrival** *(Diffused)* | Post-launch reflection. The mood after the boat actually crosses the river — not the launch itself, the day after. | *"It turns out the audit layer is the part that makes the AI feel like a tool rather than a black box."* |
| **Cold position-take** *(Egocentric)* | Once-a-quarter line in the sand. Risks reading mean. | *"Yield without an agent is a hobby."* |
| **Warm-distant** *(Altruistic)* | Architect speaking to future inhabitants of a building still being finished. "For you" framing. | *"We've been building Swidge so you can switch into a new L2 without thinking about how. MegaETH is the latest one in."* |
| **Withdrawal-as-discipline** *(Unsociable)* | Doesn't argue the discourse, just leaves it. Best paired with a separate Ecosystem-warmth post the same day. | *"While the timeline debates which L2 has the best meta-narrative, Swidge now bridges into MegaETH from any chain. We'll see you when you actually want to use it."* |

The other 12 tempi in the framework — the 4 Near Variations (Materialistic, Human, Warm, Cool), the 4 Mobile outer-action tempi (Unacknowledged, Acknowledged, Revealed, Concealed), and the 4 Awake outer-action tempi (Acute, Doubting, Certain, Uncertain) — aren't accessible to Infinex's placement under its current stress mechanics. The reason is structural: Flow-stress in Stable+Penetrating projects outward through Adream and Remote (which is where the 7 beat-only registers above come from). Time-stress in Stable+Penetrating would project through Near and Awake instead — and that's exactly the placement ProjectJin occupies. So those 12 tempi are *structural siblings'* territory: ProjectJin's (Time-stressed Stable) for the Near + Awake outer-actions, Cream's (Near baseline) for the Near Variations. Not absent in some absolute sense — accessed by adjacent characters in our brand family via different stress mechanics.

## A structural note: Infinex's character family

For anyone curious about how Infinex relates to other characters in the same family:

- **Cream** is *Near + Circumscribing + Flow-stressed, Doing+Passion.* A completely different baseline. The "newspaper-guy meme historian, Pokemon card collector, natively rich, fucking loves memes" voice. Lives in the body, in the present, in delight.
- **ProjectJin** is *Stable + Penetrating + Time-stressed, Doing+Passion + Vision-access.* **The same inner attitude and aspect as Infinex, one stress-axis apart.** The "Nick Fury × Circus MC × spy-agency overseer" voice. Operational command of chaos, dry edge.

The structural twin worth knowing: **when Infinex slips into Time-stressed urgency, it literally sounds like ProjectJin.** Same inner attitude. Same aspect. Different stress. So if a draft reads operational/urgent rather than patient/destination-pointing, you're probably borrowing the ProjectJin character by accident.

## Appendix — for the curious

The character above is derived from Mirodan's character-placement adaptation of Laban Movement Analysis. Infinex's locked placement is *Stable + Penetrating + Flow-stress (bound pole), Spell→Vision (Diagram D)*. The five named registers correspond to specific tempo cells in that placement plus its three outer-attitude projections (Adream + Remote). The most common alternative register seen in the pre-lock corpus maps to *Stable + Penetrating + Time-stress, Doing→Vision* — same baseline character, one stress axis off.

The lock was established in May 2026. The corpus measured against it (380 samples) was produced before the lock existed. Treat all backward-looking numbers as baseline observations, not performance reviews.
