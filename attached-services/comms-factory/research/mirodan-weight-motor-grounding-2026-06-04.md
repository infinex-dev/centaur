## HEADLINE (read this first)

1. **"Unhedged declarative certainty = Strong Weight" is a CATEGORY ERROR.** It is not grounded
   anywhere in the corpus. Weight is defined purely as *muscular force / contraction against
   resistance* (Sensing, "the impact of receiving and transmitting sensory stimuli", Ch1 p. 316).
   Declarative certainty / absence of hedging is a property of **Space (Directness)** and of **Time
   (Quick/Sustained Deciding)** and of the *attitude of certainty* â€” none of which is Weight. Mirodan
   explicitly warns against exactly this confusion: "we often confuse Thinking and Attending with
   quick, direct, unhesitating decision taking" (Ch1 p. 298), and there is an entire Awake variation â€”
   **"certain"** (Direct + Sustained, p. 481) â€” that carries declarative certainty with **NO Weight
   pole at all**. Certainty without force is canonical, named, and Weightless.

2. **The four LIGHT working actions are FLOATING, FLICKING, GLIDING, DABBING.** The four STRONG are
   WRINGING, SLASHING, PRESSING, PUNCHING (Ch1 p. 341, table reproduced verbatim below). Confirmed.

3. **Highest-value grounded calibration rule:** *Read Weight ONLY from force/effort/contraction
   evidence, never from confidence, finality, or unhedged assertion.* Strong Weight requires evidence
   of "contraction of the central body muscles" / exertion against resistance (Ch1 p. 316, 320). A
   sentence can be maximally certain, direct, and final and still be **Light** (or Weight-neutral). The
   classifier's collapse to "commanding" (Strong + Direct) is being driven by reading Directness +
   finality and *mislabeling the Strong pole*; the corpus says certainty/finality belong to Space and
   the Awake "certain"/Stable "self-contained" cells, not to Weight.

## Q1 â€” WEIGHT factor: markers of Strong vs Light, and is "unhedged = Strong" grounded?

**Headline: Weight = muscular force/contraction against resistance (gravity). Strong vs Light is read
from EXERTION, not from confidence or declarative tone. "Unhedged = Strong Weight" is NOT FOUND and is
contradicted by the corpus â€” it is a category error confusing Space-Directness / certainty-attitude
with the Weight effort.**

Defining passages (Ch1, "WEIGHT (Sensing/Intending)", pp. 316â€“318):

> "The dynamic motion factor which expresses the Mental Factor of Sensing and the Inner Participation
> of Intending; the impact of receiving and transmitting sensory stimuli â€” subdivided into the
> Elements of Light and Strong." ("Book" p. 11, q. Ch1 p. 316)

> "Light Intending â€” a light, sensory, muscular exertion which does not involve contraction of the
> central body muscles." ("Book" p. 6, q. Ch1 p. 316)

> "Strong Intending â€” a sensory muscular firmness in any part of the body, and emanating from the
> contraction of the central body muscles." ("Book" p. 6, q. Ch1 p. 316)

The basis for Weight as force/pressure/exertion against resistance â€” **CONFIRMED verbatim**:

> "All our senses work on the basis of an interface between specific body tissues and the environment.
> Thus all senses can be said to be a form of 'touch'â€¦ The reaction of our sensory tissues to this
> 'touch' is a form of counter-pressure, measured, ultimately, in terms of gravity. Hence Laban's
> association of Sensing with Weight." (Ch1 p. 292)

> "Resistance can be internal (antagonistic muscles) OR external (obstacles in the play)." (Ch1 p. 317)

Strong = downwards / giving weight; Light = upwards / lifting away from gravity (Ch1 p. 318). Physical
centre of the contraction = the abdominal muscles tied to the 12 dorsal vertebrae. So the *evidence*
for Strong Weight is: muscular firmness, contraction, pushing against an obstacle, giving in to /
fighting gravity. The evidence for Light is: exertion without central-muscle contraction, lift,
buoyancy.

**Is there ANY canonical basis for reading declarative certainty / absence of hedging / categorical
assertion as Strong Weight? â€” NO. NOT FOUND, and actively contradicted.**

- Certainty/finality is the province of **Space (Direct vs Flexible)** and **Time (Sustained vs
  Quick)**, not Weight. Direct Attending is "intentive (eager, earnest, penetrating)â€¦ traverses a
  straight lineâ€¦ in a single plane" (Ch1 p. 321). Flexible is "tentative, half-realisedâ€¦ hesitant,
  circumspect." So **hedging vs non-hedging maps onto Space (Flexible vs Direct), not Weight.** An
  unhedged categorical assertion is evidence of **Direct Space**, full stop.

- Mirodan explicitly flags the confusion: "we often confuse Thinking and Attending with quick, direct,
  unhesitating decision taking" â€” and says they are NOT that (Ch1 p. 298).

- The decisive counter-example: the **Awake "certain"** variation is **Direct + Sustained** (Space +
  Time), motor Pressing/Gliding, "Slow dawning of a 'certain awareness'" (p. 481). It is by definition
  **Weightless** â€” Awake has no Intending/Weight component at all (p. 474). A maximally *certain* read
  is canonically achievable with ZERO Strong-Weight. Likewise Stable **"self-contained"** is
  **Light + Direct** (p. 497) â€” declarative, direct, controlled, and **Light**.

- Strong characters are described via sensual warmth and bulk, not certainty: Othello, Sir Toby Belch
  = "high Weight (sensuous warmth, large stage volume)" (Ch1 p. 318). The marker of Strong is sensual
  mass and exertion, never tone of conviction.

Conclusion: **declarative certainty is orthogonal to Weight.** Treating "unhedged prose" as a Strong-
Weight signal is precisely the category error the calibration task suspected. The unhedged-ness is a
Space (Direct) signal and/or an Awake-certain / Stable-self-contained attitude signal. It says nothing
about force.

## Q3 â€” Method: how an observer reads a tempo from evidence

**Headline: GROUNDED. Mirodan gives an explicit observer method (Laban's four questions), an explicit
"which factors are load-bearing" rule, an explicit rule against collapsing multiple present factors,
and an explicit rule that the non-baseline Action Attitudes (Mobile/Remote/Awake) surface as
PROJECTIONS of a baseline under stress and must not be read as homes.**

**Laban's four observation questions** (Ch1 p. 310) â€” the literal reading procedure:
> - which body part moves and how does it co-ordinate?
> - duration / relation to surrounding durations? [Time]
> - muscular power exerted? [Weight]
> - direction in space? [Space]

Note "muscular power exerted?" is the Weight question â€” again confirming Weight is read from *exertion*,
not tone.

**Don't collapse the factor terms / don't read Weight in isolation** (Ch1 p. 312):
> "the words Weight, Space, Time and Flow have no expressive power in themselvesâ€¦ they are codes which
> describe a psychological experienceâ€¦ These terms are abstract conventions, like writing H2O for
> water. Don't separate Motion Factors from their Inner Participation roots."

**Inner Attitude is read from TWO factors (Shadow Moves, 2-axis); Action Attitude / Drive from THREE
(full Working Actions, visible).** This is the load-bearing structural rule for *how many* factors to
name (Ch3 p. 530):
> "The Externalized Drives are expressed 'fully' through three Motion Factors creating complete Working
> Actions. The Inner Attitudesâ€¦ are only expressed through two 'incomplete' Motion Factors, in the form
> of Shadow Moves."

So a tempo (Variation) is named on **exactly the two Motion Factors that constitute its Inner
Attitude** â€” e.g. Stable Variations are read on Weight Ă— Space ONLY (p. 487 Variations table). Time and
Flow can be *present as stress/motor evidence* but are NOT part of the Stable tempo name. The classifier
must not let a present-but-non-constituent factor pull the label.

**Baseline vs Action Attitudes â€” how the non-baseline ones surface** (Ch2 p. 503-504 footnote, p. 432
fn, p. 474 fn; reference Â§3):
> "Mobile, like Awake and Remote, not having Intending as a component, can only exist as an Action
> Attitude itself. We therefore speak of Mobile 'states' or 'tempi', not of Mobile Inner Attitudes or
> Charactersâ€¦ Mobile can only exist as an aspect of the Action of Characters which are either Adream
> (Time-stressed) or Near (Flow-stressed)."

So Mobile/Remote/Awake tempi are read as **transient projections** of a Stable/Near/Adream baseline
under a specific Stress â€” never as the resting home. The "swing of the pendulum" / multi-Variation rule
(Ch2 p. 470): a character "use[s] at least three of the four 'tempi'" and can switch Stress within a
scene to create "beats." This grounds the classifier's `outer_action_tempi` array concept â€” secondary
tempi DO flash within a sample â€” but constrains it: an Action-Attitude tempo read as the *whole-copy
primary* is illegal (it has no Weight, can't be a baseline).

**The analyst direction** (Ch1 p. 337): the movement analyst journeys *from observed involuntary
gesture â†’ to personality*; the actor reverses it. The Director (our classifier) is doing the analyst's
direction: read the visible result, infer the placement. (Reference Â§11 confirms this is Mirodan's
explicit method framing.)

## Q4 â€” "Commanding" and its confusable neighbours

**Headline: GROUNDED. Commanding = Stable, Strong + Direct (Weight + Space), motor Punching/Pressing,
"bold resolve" (p. 494). The tempi most easily MISREAD as commanding are the ones that share Directness
and/or finality but lack the Strong-Weight: Stable "self-contained" (Light+Direct), Awake "certain"
(Direct+Sustained, Weightless), and Remote "egocentric/sociable" (Direct + Flow). The differentiating
factor in every case is WEIGHT â€” and Weight is exactly the factor the corpus says you cannot read from
declarative tone.**

Commanding, defining passage (Ch2 p. 494):
> "Commanding demonstration or acceptance of a 'bold resolve'. Motifs: Punching/Pressing." Most
> contending Stable Variation. Edith Cavell statue; Solness; Zarastro.

Factor shape: **Strong + Direct** (Stable Variations table, p. 487). Both Strong-Weight AND Direct-
Space are required.

Adjacent tempi and the factor on which each differs from commanding:

| Tempo | Attitude | Factor shape | Differs from commanding on | What gets misread |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Self-contained** | Stable | **Light** + Direct (p. 497) | **WEIGHT** (Light not Strong) | A controlled, direct, declarative-but-not-forceful line. "Cautious expression of a gentle deference", motor Gliding/Dabbing. Direct + final, but Light. |
| **Certain** | Awake (outer) | Direct + **Sustained** (Weight-LESS) (p. 481) | **WEIGHT absent entirely** + Time | Pure declarative certainty about an idea/fact. "Slow dawning of a certain awareness", motor Pressing/Gliding. The classic "this is just true" line â€” Space+Time, no Weight. |
| **Practical** | Stable | Strong + **Flexible** (p. 495) | **SPACE** (Flexible not Direct) | A strong line that reasons / wavers / qualifies its route. Motor Wringing/Slashing. |
| **Egocentric / Sociable** | Remote (outer) | Direct + **Flow** (Bound/Free) (p. 442/447) | **WEIGHT replaced by FLOW** | A curt, direct, withdrawn refusal (egocentric) or a warm, direct sociability â€” Direct, but the second factor is Flow, not Weight. |

The three "what is being misread as commanding" archetypes the task asked for, grounded:

- **Light + Direct (self-contained):** Stable Light/Direct, "gentle deference", Gliding/Dabbing,
  "upright Victorian lady tempoâ€¦ light Dabbing physically, nasty underneath" (p. 497-498). Distinguished
  from commanding by Weight: it is direct and controlled but exerts no force.

- **Space + Time without Weight (Awake "certain"):** Direct + Sustained, Weightless (p. 481, 474).
  "characters in Awake mode give the feeling of being sensuously cold and emotionally neutralâ€¦ solid,
  static, light and transparent: like glass, like crystal" (p. 475). This is the canonical home of
  *declarative certainty with no force* â€” the single most likely thing being misclassified as
  commanding by an "unhedged = Strong" heuristic.

- **Remote Space + Flow (sociable):** Direct + Free, motor Gliding/Dabbing, "Developing or contracting
  feeling of 'sociable companionship'" (p. 447). Distinguished from commanding by carrying Flow instead
  of Weight â€” it is direct but relational/yielding, not forceful.

The through-line: **commanding's distinguishing claim is the STRONG-WEIGHT pole.** Every confusable
neighbour shares Direct-Space (and often finality) but substitutes Light-Weight, no-Weight, or Flow.
If the only evidence the classifier has is "direct + categorical + unhedged", that evidence is fully
satisfied by self-contained / certain / sociable â€” and Weight is unproven. The corpus mandates reading
Strong-Weight from independent force evidence; absent that, commanding is the wrong default.

## GROUNDED CALIBRATION RULES (add these to the classifier prompt)

Each rule carries its citation. These are safe to add.

1. **Read Weight ONLY from force/exertion/contraction evidence.** Strong = "contraction of the central
   body muscles", muscular firmness, pushing against resistance, sensual mass/warmth; Light = exertion
   without central contraction, lift, buoyancy. (Ch1 p. 316, 318, 292, 320.) In prose: Strong reads as
   accumulating weight-bearing pressure, heavy landing, force against an obstacle â€” NOT as confidence.

2. **Declarative certainty / absence of hedging is a SPACE (Direct) signal, not a Weight signal.**
   Direct = "intentiveâ€¦ straight line, single plane"; Flexible = "tentative, half-realised, hesitant."
   Hedging vs categorical maps onto Flexible vs Direct. (Ch1 p. 321, 298.) Do not upgrade an unhedged
   line to Strong-Weight on the strength of its certainty.

3. **A certain/final/declarative line with no force evidence is Awake "certain" (Direct+Sustained,
   Weightless) or Stable "self-contained" (Light+Direct), NOT commanding.** Test for independent Strong-
   Weight evidence before choosing commanding. (Ch2 p. 481, 497; Awake is Weightless, p. 474.)

4. **Reconcile the named motor with the Weight pole â€” they cannot contradict.** Gliding, Dabbing,
   Floating, Flicking are LIGHT by definition; Wringing, Slashing, Pressing, Punching are STRONG.
   (Ch1 p. 341.) If the motor is a Light working action, the Weight pole is Light; do not emit
   Strong + {gliding|dabbing|floating|flicking}. **GROUNDED â€” the proposed reconciliation rule is
   valid.**

5. **Name a tempo on EXACTLY the two Motion Factors that constitute its Inner Attitude.** Stable on
   WeightĂ—Space only; Near on WeightĂ—Time; Adream on WeightĂ—Flow; Mobile on TimeĂ—Flow; Remote on
   SpaceĂ—Flow; Awake on SpaceĂ—Time. Stress/motor are supporting evidence, not extra naming factors.
   Don't collapse all present factors into one label. (Ch1 p. 312, p. 487 table; Ch3 p. 530.)
   *(This rule is already present and correct in `actor-memory.ts` Director method â€” see below.)*

6. **Inner Attitude = 2 factors; Action Attitude / Drive = 3 factors.** Read 2-factor for the resting
   tempo, 3rd factor (Stress) only when a visible Action projection is present. (Ch3 p. 530.)

7. **Mobile / Remote / Awake tempi cannot be whole-copy primaries** â€” they lack Weight/Intending and
   exist only as projections of a Stable/Near/Adream baseline under stress. (Ch2 p. 503-504, 432, 474.)

8. **A sample can carry 3 of 4 Variations + Stress-switches as transient "beats."** Secondary tempi
   legitimately flash; record them as outer-action moments, not as the primary. (Ch2 p. 470.)
