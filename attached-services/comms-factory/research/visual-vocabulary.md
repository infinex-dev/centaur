# Visual vocabulary — Wave 1 recon synthesis

**For:** Rambo (Remotion templates), and anyone scoping Infinex's comms-factory visual surface.
**Based on:** Wave 1 recon of Phantom, Hyperliquid, Pendle, Polymarket, Berachain, Monad. See `wave-1/*.md`.
**Confidence:** medium overall. Twitter scraping was blocked across all six recons — motion-vocabulary sections were reconstructed from blogs, landing pages, agency case studies, and secondhand coverage. Specifics flagged at the end.

---

## TL;DR

Every serious crypto brand we studied runs a **one-accent-on-near-black** color system with **bespoke or editorial-grade type** and **restrained motion**. The crypto-native baseline for "non-slop" is now: monochrome plus one, type does the heavy lifting, motion serves the message. Where the brands diverge is on two axes — **data-hero vs. lore-hero** and **single-register vs. two-register**. Infinex's empty quadrant is **data-hero × two-register**: no one in this set occupies it, and it fits "grown-up sophisticated, in-on-the-joke without being mean" applied to a wallet/exchange with rich telemetry.

---

## What every brand in the set already shares

Not differentiators anymore — the new baseline. Match them or look amateur.

1. **One accent color on near-black.** Phantom `#AB9FF2` purple. Hyperliquid mint teal (~`#97FCE4`). Polymarket `#2E5CFF` blue. Berachain honey-gold ~`#F5B82E`. Monad `#6E54FF` electric purple. Pendle is the exception — they run a two-color *semantic* system (`#1BE3C2` PT-green = principal, `#7AB7FF` YT-blue = yield) — but the discipline is the same: every saturated pixel earns its meaning.

2. **Bespoke or editorial type.** Phantom commissioned F37 Foundry to cut a custom face. Hyperliquid runs a serif as display (rare in crypto). Pendle paid for Kyrial Sans Pro. Monad runs Britti Sans + Roboto Mono used decoratively. Polymarket is Inter-only — that's now the floor, not the ceiling. Plain Inter on its own reads as default; pair it with an unexpected secondary or it ages instantly.

3. **Restrained motion, no crypto-stock-cliché.** Across the set: no glitch, no CRT scanline, no Matrix rain, no rainbow conic gradient, no neon glow, no Tron grid, no five-color brand soup. Motion serves the message — type reveals, draw-in charts, mascot beats — never decoration.

4. **Product/chain-agnostic discipline.** Phantom is the loudest: every announcement is purple, regardless of which chain is launching. Hyperliquid never breaks into "the perp color" or "the spot color." Infinex is multi-chain and multi-venue — refusing to color-code chains is the move; chain references live as text/logo labels inside Infinex's palette, not as accent swings.

## The 2 axes that split the set

|  | **Data-hero** | **Lore-hero** |
|--|---------------|---------------|
| **Single-register** | Polymarket, Pendle, Hyperliquid | Phantom |
| **Two-register** | *empty* | Monad, Berachain |

- **Data-hero** — the live product is the comms artifact. Polymarket = screenshot of a market card. Pendle = yield split diagram. Hyperliquid = trading interface restraint. Phantom is the outlier — mascot-and-identity-driven, not data-driven.
- **Lore-hero** — serialized vocabulary, recurring motifs, mascot- or vibe-as-comms. Berachain's bears + Q5 + Honeypaper. Monad's Monanimals + Cards drop. Phantom's ghost (mascot but no broader lore arc).
- **Single-register** — one brand voice across all comms. Sophistication only, no shitpost lane.
- **Two-register** — official + cultural, both authorized. Monad runs Britti-Sans-clean-perf-claim alongside MS-Paint-Monanimal-meme. Berachain runs Honeypaper-editorial alongside Smokey-in-a-bear-mask. Tied together by a single visual anchor (Monad's purple, Berachain's honey).

## Infinex's empty quadrant — data-hero × two-register

The natural fit:

- Infinex is a wallet/exchange with rich on-chain data (balances, swaps, bridges, yields, portfolio deltas) — **data-hero is high-ROI** because every comms artifact can be a real product moment, distributable as a screenshot the same way Polymarket's chart-card has become its own meme template (entire third-party industry of "fake Polymarket screenshot" generators exists).
- The brief calls for "grown-up sophisticated, in-on-the-joke without being mean" — **two registers carry this better than one**, because forcing the joke into the sophisticated template dilutes both. Better: one template for serious launches, one for in-on-the-joke moments, sharing one identifying primitive (color + wordmark + type).
- No one in the studied set occupies this quadrant. That's the position.

## Template families to sketch first

What goes into Remotion before anything else:

### 1. The "data card" template (official register)
Polymarket's gift to the category. Take a live Infinex data point — a swap rate, a bridge progress, a portfolio delta, a yield % — and treat it as the protagonist. Big-numeric in the display face, single accent color, a one-line caption, a small icon. Every video is a screenshot waiting to be re-shared. **Build this template family first.** The product already produces the content; the template just frames it.

### 2. The "data wry" template (cultural register)
Same data card, but the caption does the in-on-the-joke work. Same accent, same type, same chrome — different copy register. **The chart stays sober; the joke lives in the caption.** This is how Polymarket-style data-hero meets Monad-style two-register without bolting on a mascot.

### 3. The "launch-tier" template (reserved, ~4× per year)
Berachain's lesson: a separate template that ONLY gets used for major events, with a reserved sub-palette and a reserved typographic move that signals "this one matters." Different accent within Infinex's system, different aspect ratio, different motion primitive (e.g., big-type-reveal vs. the data-card's chart-draw-in). The scarcity is the signal.

### 4. The "split" template
Pendle's lesson. If Infinex has any product motion that's semantically a split (in/out, bridge-from/bridge-to, principal/yield, spot/perp, on-chain/off-chain), build one template that visualizes the split with a reserved color pair. The split becomes Infinex's most legible visual primitive after the wordmark.

**Defer (do NOT build yet):** 3D mascot, character animation, illustrated explainer, scroll-cinema, anything that requires a serialized lore substrate Infinex doesn't have yet.

## What to steal — one technique per brand

- **Phantom** — chain-agnostic discipline. Chain references as labels inside Infinex's accent system, not as accent swings.
- **Hyperliquid** — one unexpected display face. Theirs is serif (now claimed); Infinex's could be a high-contrast didone, a stencil, a humanist condensed, or a slab. One unexpected typographic move buys more sophistication than VFX.
- **Pendle** — semantic color. Pick two saturated colors that always mean the same two things across every template. Lock them.
- **Polymarket** — data-as-poster. Every video is a screenshot of a live Infinex moment. Embed-distributable is a principle, not a feature.
- **Berachain** — serialization mechanic. One "launch-tier" template gets used 4× per year. The scarcity signals the moment.
- **Monad** — two-register discipline. One color anchor, two voices. Commission the cultural register from brand-factory; don't fake bottom-up.

## What to avoid — nine specific traps

1. **Variable-weight type animation** (Phantom signature) — now reads as "I hired a fintech studio circa 2023."
2. **Cinematic smooth-scroll marketing-site cosplay in Remotion** — wrong medium; collapses video into website-feel.
3. **Crypto-neon glow / cyberpunk caustics / Tron grids** (Hyperliquid avoids) — universally dated.
4. **Five-color gradient brand soup** (Hyperliquid avoids) — the inherited 2021-era L1 default.
5. **Mascot-as-required-presence** (Monad avoids) — Duolingo-owl energy if forced.
6. **Maximalist motion on perf/data claims** (Monad avoids) — over-animating the counter undercuts credibility.
7. **Meme-format posts** (Pendle avoids) — Wojaks/Chad-virgin reads as condescension to a sophisticated audience.
8. **Lore vocabulary without illustration system** (Berachain avoids) — text-only shitposts without recurring visual anchors read as cringe.
9. **Specific claimed palettes** — avoid `#2E5CFF` (Polymarket), `#1BE3C2` (Pendle PT), `#AB9FF2` (Phantom), `#6E54FF` (Monad), and the mint-teal range ~`#97FCE4` (Hyperliquid). Honey-gold ~`#F5B82E` is Berachain-coded.
