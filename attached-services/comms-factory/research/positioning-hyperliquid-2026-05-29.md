**The noun matters:** they claim a *place / infrastructure* ("house", "rails", "blockchain"), not a product ("the best perp DEX"). Perps were the wedge; the claimed slot is settlement infrastructure for *every* asset class.

## Who they are (identity) vs. what they do (product surface)

- **Identity:** a performant L1 blockchain (HyperBFT consensus; HyperCore order-book engine + HyperEVM general compute) operated by Hyperliquid Labs / Hyper Foundation. Self-image: the most transparent, fully-onchain financial system — "returning to the core ethos of crypto." Built with no external funding, all protocol fees to the community.
- **Product surface:** an onchain order-book exchange (perps + spot), plus permissionless primitives that let *others* build the rest: HIP-1 token standard, HIP-3 permissionless perp deployment, HIP-4 outcome/prediction markets, builder codes (Phantom, Ledger integrations), RWA/commodity perps, native USDC/USDH.

**How each ship ladders into the claim:** every primitive widens the set of finance that can live on-chain. Perps → spot → permissionless derivatives → RWAs/commodities → outcome markets → institutional settlement. The throughline in their own copy: each release is framed as "a step towards housing all of finance." The claim is a roadmap container, and they explicitly map ships into it.

## Implicit positioning statement

> For traders, builders, and (increasingly) institutions who want financial markets that are fully onchain, transparent, and permissionless, **Hyperliquid is the blockchain that houses all of finance** — every asset class, every step of the lifecycle (build a project, launch a token, trade it) on one verifiable chain — unlike centralized venues (Binance) and opaque competitors (Lighter, Aster) whose execution can't be independently verified, and unlike single-product DEXs that only do perps.

## Distinctive verbal / structural moves

- **Metric-as-protagonist ships.** The dominant template is a single ATH stat in declarative present tense: "Open interest has reached new all-time highs seven days in a row." No adjectives, no "thrilled to." The number is the news.
- **Ladder line.** High-signal posts close by tying the stat back to the mission: "This is an important step towards housing all of finance." The category claim is restated as the recurring outro.
- **Negation-as-positioning.** "On Hyperliquid, there is no listing fee, no listing department, and no gatekeepers." They define themselves by what they refuse (gatekeepers, opacity, VC funding).
- **Receipts over rhetoric.** Their biggest posts are a point-by-point onchain rebuttal of an FUD article and a zero-downtime stress-test brag — transparency/verifiability as the core differentiator, argued with chain data, not adjectives.
- **"By community request..."** standard opener for new-market listings — frames product expansion as demand-pull, not push.
- **Restraint as signal.** No emoji-spam, no hype verbs, no gradient soup. Editorial-serif + one-mint-accent visual identity reinforces "grown-up, performance-as-taste" (see research/wave-1/hyperliquid.md).
