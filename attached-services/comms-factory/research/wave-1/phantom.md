## Visual fingerprint (1 tight paragraph)
Phantom looks like a sophisticated consumer fintech that happens to be crypto, not the other way around. The system runs on a single confident purple (#AB9FF2) sitting on near-black, paired with iridescent / holographic accents that show up on hero objects (the Visa card in the Cash launch, occasional dimensional ghost renders). Type is a bespoke F37 Zagma derivative ("Phantom") — geometric sans with quiet personality moves (no tail on the lowercase a, horizontal bar on the 4, custom ink-traps that echo the wordmark); it reads as restrained, not expressive, and does the heavy lifting through tightly-tracked weight contrasts and a variable axis they actually animate. The ghost is the only "character" in the system and it's rendered as a clean vector volume — soft, sleek, mascot-as-companion rather than mascot-as-joke. Across comms the density is low, the surface is dark, the motion is springy-but-disciplined (Lottie + Framer Motion, linear-interp scroll, bouncy logo micro-interactions tied to product data like transaction direction). Attitude is *grown-up crypto-native* — they are confidently a consumer brand, with zero exchange-potshot energy, zero memey shitposting in the brand assets, and the in-on-the-joke moments live inside the ghost's facial expressions and posture, not the copy.

## Motion vocabulary
- **Transitions** — masked reveals on scroll (sections appear under a clipping mask rather than fading in), springy logo morphs, and Lottie-driven ghost beats between scenes. Not cuts; almost everything is continuous. Scroll itself is the dominant transition device (linear-interpolated smooth scroll on the marketing site).
- **Element motion** — spring-based easing for the mascot, linear-interp for scroll-tied parallax, weight/axis interpolation on the variable type (they animate the typeface itself heavier/lighter). Static UI screenshots are framed inside motion contexts (the device drifts, the screen content scrolls) rather than presented as flat stills. No jitter, no chaotic stagger.
- **Tempo** — measured. Not cinematic-slow, not fast-cut social-punch. The brand motion sits in a "confident demo" register — closer to Apple keynote pacing than to crypto-Twitter quick cuts. Long hold on hero elements, short snap on state changes.
- **Sound role** — silent-friendly. The flagship surfaces (marketing site, blog hero loops) read clearly muted; product-launch videos on YT use music-anchored beds rather than VO-driven. Nothing in their on-feed comms suggests sound is required for the message to land.

## Type & color discipline
- **Typefaces** — "Phantom" (custom, by F37 Foundry, a modification of F37 Zagma). Variable weight axis is treated as a design tool, not just a setting. Mono usage is minimal-to-absent in marketing; UI uses a system sans.
- **Type personality** — confident geometric sans with restrained personality cuts. Not austere (the round wordmark warmth balances it), not expressive (no flourishes, no display). It's the "we built our own typeface because we're a serious consumer brand" move, executed quietly.
- **Palette** — Primary brand purple `#AB9FF2` (confirmed in their public React SDK theme as the canonical action color). Surfaces are predominantly dark/near-black with deep purple-to-blue gradients on hero compositions. Holographic / iridescent treatments appear as accents on physical objects (the Visa card in Phantom Cash). Whites are used as type-on-dark, not as background.
- **Color discipline** — restrained-monochromatic. One hero color, one surface, accents earned by the object (a card's iridescence, the ghost's volumetric shading) rather than added by the layout. They do not chain-color-code (Solana ≠ purple, Ethereum ≠ blue) in marketing comms — chains are referenced as text labels, the visual language stays Phantom-purple regardless of which chain the announcement is about. This is a *real* discipline call, not a default.

## 3 visual anti-patterns
1. **The dimensional / iridescent ghost.** Phantom owns volumetric-ghost-as-mascot. Any Infinex mascot or character work that lands in the same "clean vector volume with soft shading" register will read as derivative. The mascot itself is the trap, not just its color.
2. **Variable-type-as-motion.** Animating type weight along a variable axis is a signature Bakken & Bæck move (Phantom, Ramp, others). Hot in 2023; now reads as "I hired a fintech rebrand studio circa 2023." Use sparingly or it telegraphs the reference.
3. **Smooth-scroll-with-linear-interp marketing site as the comms hero.** Phantom's web is the brand spine. For a *comms-factory* pipeline shipping short videos, copying the smooth-scroll cinematic register is a category error — the medium is wrong, and trying to retrofit it into Remotion makes everything look like a marketing-site demo rather than comms.

## Steal vs. avoid (for Rambo)
Rambo — the one move worth porting is **chain-agnostic visual discipline**. Phantom is a multi-chain wallet that has aggressively refused to chain-color-code its comms (no Solana-purple here, Eth-blue there, Base-blue when Base launches). Every announcement looks like *Phantom*, regardless of which network the news is about. For Infinex — which is also cross-chain and cross-venue — this is the most directly portable lesson: build the Remotion templates around Infinex's own palette and let chain references live as text/logo labels inside the existing color system, not as accent colors that swing per post. **The trap:** do not import their *specific* moves to enforce this — no variable-type weight animation, no springy mascot, no clean-vector ghost surrogate, no dark-purple-gradient hero. Those are Phantom's signatures and will read as imitation. Take the *principle* (one brand color, accents come from the object not the layout, type does the heavy lifting), leave the *executions* (the ghost, the variable type, the smooth-scroll cinema). If you need one concrete technique to lift: their use of **dark surface + single hero color + object-derived iridescent accents** is a transferable formula — swap Phantom's purple for whatever brand-factory lands on, and find an Infinex-native "object" to carry the iridescence (a card, a coin, a wrapped asset rendered with the same dimensional care). Avoid the ghost-shaped hole.
