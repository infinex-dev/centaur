**Recon scope:** Pendle brand guide (full extraction of palette + typography), pendle.finance landing copy, boros.pendle.finance + pendle.finance/boros, Pendle docs (V2 + Academy + Boros), Brandfetch, ThreadReaderApp aggregation of @pendle_fi threads, Boros Medium launch post (text only), web search across Twitter/YouTube/launch coverage. **Could not directly access:** rendered Twitter/X (paywalled and 402'd via WebFetch), web.archive.org snapshots (blocked from this env), YouTube channel page (returned only footer), and the in-app interface (current build is error-state on anonymous load). Visual examples below are anchored to URLs Rambo can open in a browser; descriptions of motion are inferred from copy + brand guide + thread aggregation, not direct frame-by-frame observation.

**Recency:** Brand guide is the current published version. Threads sampled span late 2025 through May 2026 (STRC TVL announcement 2026-05-11 confirmed live; Boros launched Aug 2025). Brand kit itself appears stable across the V2 → Boros expansion.

**Confidence:** Medium. High on palette / typography / structural visual logic (brand guide is unambiguous and the PT/YT color system is everywhere). Medium-low on motion vocabulary specifically — Twitter media and YouTube thumbnails were not directly viewable in this environment, so motion claims are reasoned from screenshot-heavy thread descriptions and the brand kit's static-first posture, not from actually watching a Pendle motion piece.

## Visual fingerprint (1 tight paragraph)

Pendle's visual identity is a **two-color semantic system masquerading as a brand**. PT Green (#1BE3C2 — a cold teal-mint, not a DeFi-bro neon green) and YT Blue (#7AB7FF — a soft sky blue) aren't decoration; they're the protocol's typography. Every diagram, every yield card, every explainer leans on the green-vs-blue split because that split *is* the product (principal vs yield). Surround them with Pendle OG Blue (#1E4480) on near-black water grays and you get the dominant mood: a quiet, slightly clinical, **bond-desk-on-a-dark-monitor** feel — closer to a Bloomberg Terminal moodboard than a meme-coin launch. Typography is split three ways with discipline: Kyrial Sans Pro for the wordmark only (purchased license — a flex), Inter for the app, Poppins for marketing. There is **no mascot, no anime character, no recurring illustrated metaphor** beyond the two-token split diagram. The audience is assumed sophisticated; the comms refuse to over-explain. Boros sub-brand keeps the same DNA but darkens further and leans on audit-badge density (ChainSecurity, Spearbit, WatchPug, all visible above-the-fold) as the dominant trust signal instead of illustration.

## Motion vocabulary

- **Transitions** — Inferred (not directly observed): clean cross-fades and value-counter tickers dominate over flashy cuts. Their primary motion artifact in threads is **the product itself moving** — APY tickers, yield curves redrawing, PT-price-converging-to-1 animations — not bespoke motion graphics layered on top. When Pendle ships motion, it tends to be the chart itself animating, framed by the brand chrome. Cinematic anime-style reveals are absent.
- **Element motion** — Two-token split is the recurring kinetic idea: a single SY token visually divides into PT (green) and YT (blue), often along a horizontal or arrow-branched axis, with the two halves continuing to move on independent timelines (PT converges to 1, YT decays to 0 — that *price decay* is itself the most Pendle-coded motion in their explainers). Timeline scrubs against a maturity date are a second recurring motion primitive.
- **Tempo** — Calm, mid-paced, no whip-cuts. Their tagline (`TradingHedgingLiberatingEarningLeveragingFixingYield` — six verbs scrolling into "Yield") suggests a deliberate **action-verb-cycler** as the dominant hero motion device, which is a slow, dignified rotation rather than a fast vertical reel. Pendle reads as "the grown-up in the room" partly because they don't rush the viewer.
- **Sound role** — None evident from accessible sources. Pendle School / product threads are screenshot-and-text on Twitter; produced videos exist on the YouTube channel (UChIaQyX3nQmgaDjmfLCGrww) but channel rendering was blocked from this recon. Assume sound is **either absent or used as a low ambient bed** consistent with a "Bloomberg terminal" mood, not as a punchline.

## Type & color discipline

- **Typefaces** — Kyrial Sans Pro (logo only, paid license), Inter (app UI, free), Poppins (marketing/advertising, free). Three-typeface split with strict context separation is itself the brand's strongest typographic move — most DeFi protocols use one font for everything; Pendle treats the logo as *sacred*, the app as *workmanlike*, and the marketing as *friendly*.
- **Type personality** — Hero text uses uppercase product-section labels (`BOROS` / `V2 SPOT`) for a quasi-institutional feel. Body type stays sentence-case and unornamented. The combination reads as "infrastructure brand built by people who read 10-Ks," not "consumer fintech."
- **Palette** — Primary: PT Green #1BE3C2, YT Blue #7AB7FF, Pendle Blue #6079FF. Accent: Success Azure #72CDDF, Accent Teal #51A69A, Guava (error) #DD5453, Gold (warning) #EFB54B. Neutrals: Pendle OG Blue #1E4480 plus two greyscale ramps — "Water" grays cascading #EBEFF5 → #090D18 (cool, blue-tinted) and "Mono" grays #F2F2F2 → #1D1B1D (true neutral). Pure white #FFFFFF in the kit but in practice the dominant surface is near-black water-gray with PT-Green and YT-Blue as the only saturated highlights.
- **Color discipline** — This is the headline finding. **Color is information, not decoration.** Green always means principal/PT; blue always means yield/YT. That semantic lock means Pendle can't be loud with color even if it wanted to be — every saturated pixel has to earn its meaning. The discipline is what makes the brand feel sophisticated; breaking it (using green decoratively for something that isn't PT) would look like a typo.

## 3 visual anti-patterns

1. **No mascot, no character, no anime cyclops.** Despite "Boros" being a One-Punch Man character (Lord Boros, cyclops, blue skin), Pendle deliberately did **not** mascot-ify their sub-brand. The name comes from the Greek for "to eat." This is a refusal-to-be-cute move and it's load-bearing for their grown-up positioning.
2. **No meme-format posts.** Their threads use product screenshots and yield comparison charts as the visual payload — not Wojaks, not Chad/virgin, not "DeFi bull run" stock imagery. When the audience is yield farmers who already get it, meme posting reads as condescension.
3. **No decorative use of brand colors.** PT-green never appears as "just a nice green accent" — it always denotes principal. If Rambo's templates use teal-mint near `#1BE3C2` for ANY purpose other than denoting a principal-like concept, it will read as a brand-color collision to anyone fluent in DeFi.

## Steal vs. avoid (for Rambo)

Rambo — the one technique worth porting is **semantic color**: pick two saturated colors that always mean the same two things across every template, and let every other element be near-black grayscale. Pendle's whole "sophisticated yield desk" vibe comes from the discipline of green-means-principal / blue-means-yield, applied so rigidly that color becomes a glossary you can read without labels. For Infinex, that pair might be "send-side / receive-side," "spot / yield," "bridge-in / bridge-out" — pick what's true to the product and lock it. The trap: don't copy *teal #1BE3C2 specifically*. That hex is now Pendle-coded in the eyes of every yield farmer who'll see Infinex comms, and adopting it will read as derivative even if the rest of the system is original. Pick a different pair (a warm hue + a cool hue, or two cool hues outside the #1BE3C2 / #7AB7FF range) and bring the *discipline* of Pendle's color-as-meaning system, not the colors themselves. Second-order trap: don't copy the verb-cycler hero either — `TradingHedgingLiberating...` was novel three years ago and is now a recognizable Pendle device; using it will signal "we studied Pendle" instead of "we're our own thing."
