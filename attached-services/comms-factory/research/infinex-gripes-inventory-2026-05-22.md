## Cross-cutting patterns the operator names

These aren't individual gripes — they're the operator's own diagnostic frames the inventory rolls up to. Listed because future audits will inherit them:

- **Single-voice vs multi-voice strategic ambiguity.** Operator has named three competing reads of Phantom (multi-voice / single-Adream-D / multi-placement casting director) and hasn't picked the response — match Phantom (multi-cast) or counter (hold tighter single-voice). Until decided, every cast-gap gripe above (IE099, the apology tweet, Sphinx replies, missing security correspondent) sits in the same unresolved decision.
- **Spec postdates corpus.** All 380 audited samples were written before any spec existed. Operator is allergic to "drifted off-spec" framing — there was nothing to drift from. The locked character is forward-looking, not a critique of past work. Treat the inventory above as forward-looking too: these are things to do *differently going forward*, not failures.
- **HTML is the living tracker.** `research/visual-vocabulary.html` is the operator's working artifact — every new finding folds in here. The gripes above that don't already appear there are candidates to add. (memory: `html-living-doc.md`)
- **Time-stress = adolescent status grammar.** Per Mirodan, time-stressed Stable reads adolescent/lower; Infinex's locked Flow-stress is mature. Every clock-pressure gripe (Sonar countdown, "Act now", "Last orders") is a status-grammar leak, not just a tonal slip.
