Key findings

1. CURRENT literally regurgitates the docstring example as card output

Two of 10 releases produced a lining that echoes the docstring example's "X wall just stopped existing" structure verbatim:

fiat-onramp lining (CURRENT):
on the surface: a new fiat deposit rail. underneath: the bank-vs-wallet wall just stopped existing for Infinex users
passkey-portable lining (CURRENT):
on the surface: passkey authentication for device portability. underneath: the seed phrase barrier to crypto adoption just stopped existing

The grounder isn't proposing — it's pattern-matching off the docstring example. This is the strongest single argument for changing card.ts.

2. Variant A swaps tokens but the GRAMMAR still echoes

"Necessary intermediate step evaporated" defeated surface-token matching (0% hit rate) but the "X just [past-tense intransitive verb]" pattern persists across A's outputs:

chain-add-megaeth lining (VARIANT_A):
on the surface: a new chain is supported. underneath: the barrier to MegaETH liquidity just collapsed.

Token cleaned. Frame and pattern preserved. Marginal improvement; not structural.

3. Variant B introduced its OWN anchor — the "refusing to..." formula

B's property-rule text says "underneath is the strategic anti-pattern the post is refusing to perform". The grounder internalized "refusing" as the lining template. Every B lining I sampled opens "underneath: refusing to...":

fiat-onramp lining (VARIANT_B):
on the surface: announcing a new deposit method. underneath: refusing to treat crypto as a separate financial realm...
chain-add-megaeth lining (VARIANT_B):
on the surface: announcing technical expansion to a new chain. underneath: refusing to treat cross-chain movement as a problem...

B traded brand-anchor for property-anchor. Less load-bearing (it's not pulling Infinex toward "walls"), but still anchoring. Worth refining for a v2 test: rewrite B's property text so it doesn't introduce "refusing" or any single verb formula.

4. Variant C decontaminates hardest BUT drops validity to 50%

Without a concrete example or structural template, the grounder produces shorter, more abstract through-actions that don't always read as proper through-actions:

yield-vault-launch (VARIANT_C): "to grant legibility" — judge flagged invalid
private-send-ga (VARIANT_C): "to graduate privacy" — judge flagged invalid
perps-maintenance (VARIANT_C): "to preserve continuity" — judge flagged invalid

C's anchoring win comes at the cost of structural integrity. In production, 5 of 10 cards would need re-grounding. Real cost.

Recommendation

Ship Variant B — with one refinement before committing.

Why B wins:

  • Anchoring drops 3.4 → 1.9 (44%) versus CURRENT — better than A (2.9) and within reach of C (1.6).
  • Validity holds at 80% — tied with A for best, materially better than C (50%).
  • Verb diversity highest at 8 distinct verbs.
  • Surface anchor hits drop to 0%.

The catch — refine before shipping: B introduced its own subtle anchor — every B lining opens "underneath: refusing to..." because B's property text says "the strategic anti-pattern the post is refusing to perform". Less load-bearing than wall/bank/dissolved, but real. Two options:

  • (a) Ship B as-is — accept "refusing to..." as a milder structural anchor. It's voice-coherent (Infinex's character refuses hype, refuses urgency theatre, etc.) so it may not hurt; might even help.
  • (b) Refine B's property text to avoid introducing any single verb formula, then re-test. Something like: "underneath: <the hidden Inner Action the prose carries — what the surface obscures>". Removes the "refusing" trigger.

Why not C: validity at 50% is a real production cost. "to grant legibility" / "to graduate privacy" / "to preserve continuity" all flagged invalid — too abstract, missing the structural-object grounding that makes a through-action playable. C is the right destination eventually, but only after we either (i) refine the canon-ref text to anchor structural form OR (ii) accept that 50% of cards need re-grounding and build a retry path.

Why not A: 2.9 anchoring is barely better than CURRENT's 3.4. Token swap defeats one anchor layer; pattern / frame / tone remain. Not worth the change.

What I'd propose next:

  • Apply Variant B to card.ts:48 and :61-62 now, with the "refusing to" trigger sentence rewritten.
  • Then proceed with the other 3 changes from research/changes-preview-2026-05-28.html (infinex.ts, actor-director.ts, generator.ts Stage A/B).
  • Optionally: run a v2 of this test with B' (the refined B) to see if anchoring drops further toward C's 1.6 without losing validity.
