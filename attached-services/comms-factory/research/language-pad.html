— A —How a single post arcs through tempi

In production every post is a beat sequence, not a single-tempo monolith. Each beat carries its own register; transitions do the emotional work. Example for "Spot Hyperliquid is now live in Infinex":

Beat 1 · Sombre · Pressing-prep

The wallet and the venue used to be separate things.
We've been taking that wall down section by section.

Beat 2 · Commanding · Punching-release

Today: spot Hyperliquid is live in Infinex.
Same account, same passkey, the orderbook where your portfolio already lives.

Beat 3 · Practical · Wringing → Slashing

Spot was harder than perps. The orderbook semantics had to feel native, not bolted on. We held it back until that was true.

Beat 4 · Irradiant · Floating → Flicking

A few months from now, you (or your agent) will route through this without thinking about it. That's the move we're making.

Sombre opens with sustained pressure (Pressing prep) → Commanding lands as decisive release (Punching, supported by Sombre's prep) → Practical justifies through tradeoff-wringing → Irradiant lifts to vision-pull. Four beats, four motors, one character moving through them.

— B —The five tempi · motors and recipes

Tempo Inner combo Motor (prep → release) Use
Commanding Stable · Strong/Direct Pressing → Punching Decisive launch landing. Always paired with a prep beat (Sombre or Practical) before it.
Practical Stable · Strong/Flexible Wringing → Slashing Justifies a build decision by working through tradeoffs.
Sombre Adream · Strong/Bound Pressing → Punching (bound) Weight-of-conviction openers. Sets up Commanding by Pressing first.
Irradiant Adream · Light/Free Floating → Flicking Vision-lift close. Future-tense agent thesis lands here.
Sociable Remote · Direct/Free Gliding → Dabbing Partner credit, ecosystem warmth. Light social touch.

The preparation hierarchy is load-bearing

Per Mirodan §1.7 (p. 347): every Quick action requires its Sustained partner as preparation. Without the prep, the Quick action degrades to its Sustained version:

Punching needs Pressing-prep — otherwise it reads as a Press
Slashing needs Wringing-prep — otherwise it reads as a Wring
Dabbing needs Gliding-prep — otherwise it reads as a Glide
Flicking needs Floating-prep — otherwise it reads as a Float

Why posts that open cold with "X IS LIVE." feel flat: they're trying to Punch without Pressing first. The Sombre/Practical opener IS the prep — pair them. The validator will enforce this; the generator will sequence beats accordingly.

01The twelve tempi · individual reference

Five locked main tempi (Commanding · Practical · Sombre · Irradiant · Sociable) plus seven beat-only tempi (rare). Reference table — fingerprint shown for each.

Stable · Light/Direct
Self-Contained
Gliding / Dabbing motifs · "cautious expression or acceptance of a gentle deference"

The Karpathy default. Confident-light. Doesn't push. Says the obvious well. Generous to the reader who's catching up. Direct, but never heavy.

Opening: label + colon · "Something useful:" · "Quick one:" — never declarative shout · Vocab: precise verbs, short nouns, "imo", concrete examples · Signoff: often none; sometimes a TLDR.

Stable · Strong/Direct
Commanding
Punching / Pressing motifs · "commanding demonstration of a bold resolve"

The institutional drop. Locked, decisive, no ornament. Reads as a ruler issuing a position. Closest to the AnthropicAI press-release register.

Opening: "We've [verb]ed X." · "X is live." · "Today: Y." · Vocab: finished verbs, named partners, no hedges · Signoff: destination link, no flourish.

Stable · Strong/Flexible
Practical
Wringing / Slashing motifs · "developing intention to cast or to submit to a spell-binding power"

The thesis-builder. Long-form. Wrings sense out of complexity. Comfortable with tradeoffs, willing to take you through the reasoning. Karpathy's essay mode.

Opening: "Here's what's interesting about X:" · "I/we keep coming back to:" · Vocab: "tradeoff", "the actual question is", "this only makes sense once you" · Signoff: open questions OR a single declarative.

Adream outer · Strong/Bound
Sombre
Pressing motifs · "overpowering sombre awareness of a staunch resolve"

The weight-of-conviction beat. Slow. Carries gravity. Used when the thesis lands with effort — "we know this is coming and it's a lot." Werle/Duke energy.

Opening: "There's a version of crypto coming where X." · "We don't think Y survives." · Vocab: "becomes", "stops being", "the gap between X and Y" · Signoff: a single image-line, not a CTA.

Adream outer · Light/Free
Irradiant
Floating / Flicking motifs · "irradiant unfolding of a sympathetic exultation"

The future-warmth beat. Vision-pulled, generous, "we're excited about the world this opens." Rare. Used when something genuinely opens up rather than just shipping.

Opening: "A few months from now..." · "What this lets you do:" · Vocab: "agent", "your own", "without thinking about it", future-tense everywhere · Signoff: short, gestural, optimistic.

Remote outer · Direct/Bound
Egocentric
Pressing motifs · "narrowing egocentric withdrawal into an unsociable solitude"

The "we don't care, we're right" beat. Cold, philosophical, distant from the market. Used sparingly — when there's a position to hold that the room won't like. Hyperliquid silence energy.

Opening: a single declarative · no preamble · Vocab: abstract nouns, no warmth · Signoff: none. The post ends mid-thought sometimes.

Stable · Light/Flexible
Receptive
Floating / Flicking motifs · "receptive acceptance or rejection of a welcoming tenderness"

The "we hear you, we're moving" beat. Open, generous, willing to incorporate. Closest to the apology-and-pivot energy of the "We got the sale wrong" post. Used after a community correction.

Opening: acknowledgment of input · "You've been telling us..." · Vocab: "we hear you", "you were right", responsiveness verbs · Signoff: a small commitment, no flourish.

Adream outer · Strong/Free
Overpowering
Wringing / Slashing motifs · "irradiant intention of casting a spell or being spell-bound"

The full-conviction-with-emotion beat. Stronger than Irradiant, looser than Sombre. Vision-pulled with emotional weight visible. Used for the rare post where conviction is breaking through restraint.

Opening: "We've been saying this for years and..." · "Here's the thing nobody else is willing to call:" · Vocab: certainty verbs, future-perfect tense · Signoff: a declarative the reader can quote.

Adream outer · Light/Bound
Diffused
Gliding / Dabbing motifs · "diffused sensation of a welcome feeling"

The "this just settled into place" beat. Quiet vision-arrival. Used for post-launch reflection, not the launch itself. The mood after the boat actually crosses the river.

Opening: a small observation · "We noticed..." · "It turns out..." · Vocab: soft verbs, "settled", "found", "turned out" · Signoff: none usually.

Remote outer · Flexible/Free
Altruistic
Floating / Flicking motifs · "embracing altruistic feeling for or against a sociable cordiality"

The "we're building this for you" beat. Warm-distant. Like the architect speaking to future inhabitants of a building they're still finishing. Used for ecosystem-thanks or community-acknowledgment moments.

Opening: "We've been building [X] so [you] can [Y]" · Vocab: "for", "so that", second-person · Signoff: hand-off to the reader, no link.

Remote outer · Direct/Free
Sociable
Gliding / Dabbing motifs · "developing or contracting feeling of sociable companionship"

The "we're with you on this one" beat. Warm-attuned. Used for partner announcements, ecosystem moments where Infinex is acknowledging being part of a larger thing. Rare but warm.

Opening: partner-name + verb · "Working with X on..." · Vocab: "together", "with", "alongside" · Signoff: a credit or thanks.

Remote outer · Flexible/Bound
Unsociable
Wringing / Slashing motifs · "growing reflective image of a welcome solitude"

The "we'll be over here building" beat. Withdrawal-as-discipline. Used when the room is loud and Infinex chooses not to engage with the noise. Different from Egocentric (which takes a position) — Unsociable just leaves the conversation.

Opening: a reflective observation about the discourse · Vocab: distance verbs, "elsewhere", "meanwhile" · Signoff: the post is the signoff.
