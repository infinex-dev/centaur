## Surface-shift notes (for downstream)

The blog voice is more *thesis-grade* than the tweet voice — longer sentences, more parallel structure, more confident assertions about industry structure. Three distinct registers stand out:

- **Numbered-update voice** (`№ N - …`) — wry, sentence-case, low ceremony. Opens with a one-line lede ("The INX token is now live."), then jumps directly into bullet-flat sections. Uses parenthetical asides ("thanks swidge", "thanks passkeys") to acknowledge product names without saying "powered by". This is the closest blog analog to Infinex's tweet announcement voice.
- **Founder-thesis voice** (Superapp Thesis, Governance Retake, Trust and Transparency) — Kain's voice, often quoted in third-person. Strong-claim openers ("Centralized exchanges are the original superapps." / "We have clear evidence that DAO governance and other forms of governance by committee, in general, are absolutely diabolical."). Italics used for term-of-art emphasis (_trustless_). When quoting Kain, the blog frames it as `"Kain observes" / "Kain doesn't mince words"` — a deliberate distancing move that lets the brand site treat Kain's positions as referenceable theses, not the brand's marching orders.
- **Errata / reflection voice** (the Yaprun pause) — measured, narrative, willing to use the word "broken" about its own program. Opens with "Crypto is a game of attention" (premise establishment), works through what they tried and why, lands on a concrete admission ("we realized the system had broken down"), then a concrete realignment ("3-5,000 verified, real people whose contributions make a difference"). Same shape as the Jan 5 apology tweet but with room to breathe — the blog format is where Infinex does its post-mortems.

The voice is consistent across years: the 2023 Litepaper ("Innovations in crypto user experience have lagged behind innovations in crypto infrastructure, and this makes sense.") sounds like it was written by the same person as the 2026 № 60 INX-is-here post. Compare to Phantom, where the founder voice and the brand voice are explicitly separated. Infinex doesn't separate them — the blog is the channel where Kain's voice and the brand voice fuse.

Also worth flagging: the blog avoids hyperbole almost completely. No "thrilled to announce", no "revolutionary", no "game-changing". The launch posts (INX is here, Perps, Keypom acquisition) are flat-declarative. The closest the blog gets to enthusiasm is "Day One support for Derive's DRV token, plus trade and win" — and that's the kind of post that's already factual on its face.
