# passkeys — sample release card

## Release card (metadata)
- name: Infinex Passkeys
- ship date: live, foundational (every Infinex account is passkey-native)
- audience: two distinct cohorts — (1) crypto-natives tired of seed-phrase ceremony, (2) crypto-curious who bounced off the seed phrase entirely and never came back
- product page: https://infinex.xyz/features#passkeys
- deployed facts:
  - "No emails, passwords, or login links. Sign in securely on any device"
  - Subhead: "Access on any device with passkeys"
  - Biometric signing (Touch ID / Face ID) for onchain transactions
  - Multiple passkeys storable per account (redundancy without compromise)
  - Cross-device sync, no security trade
  - No seed phrase

## 3 candidate data points to hero
1. **0 seed phrases** — the literal count. Whole-screen "0", small caption "seed phrases stored, written, lost, or screenshotted." This is the rare case where the absent number is the protagonist.
2. **Sign-in time delta** — how long it takes to sign a transaction with a passkey (sub-second face/touch) vs. the seed-phrase + hardware-wallet baseline. Real on-device timer overlay, recorded.
3. **N devices, 1 account** — passkeys sync. Show the same account active on phone + laptop + tablet, all biometric, no relogin. The count is the data.

## Voice notes
Passkeys is the only product in the lineup that's *philosophical* — it's an argument about what a wallet should be. Voice can be the most confident here because the technical position is correct (WebAuthn + ERC-4337 + TEEs is the future, and we're already there). But: do NOT mock people who use seed phrases — that's the audience we want to convert. The wry register should target the *artifact* (the paper, the metal plate, the screenshot in iCloud), never the person. This is also the product most likely to need the launch-tier template — passkeys is a category-defining bet, not a feature.
