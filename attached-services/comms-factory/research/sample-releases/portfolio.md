# portfolio — sample release card

## Release card (metadata)
- name: Infinex Portfolio
- ship date: live (tokens + NFTs); positions "coming soon"
- audience: multi-wallet, multi-chain holders who currently reconcile their net worth in a spreadsheet, Zapper, Debank, and three explorer tabs; the "what do I actually own" cohort
- product page: https://infinex.xyz/features#portfolio
- deployed facts:
  - "See all your tokens, NFTs (and soon positions) in one beautiful overview"
  - Rolled-up balances across wallets AND networks (single number per token, not per chain)
  - Real-time prices + price movements
  - Spam filter / token visibility management
  - Filter by wallet, network, asset type
  - Asset detail view: chart + liquidity
  - APY display for yield-earning assets

## 3 candidate data points to hero
1. **One number, every chain** — a single net-worth figure on a near-black background, no chrome. The data point is the *consolidation* itself. The wallet-count and chain-count appear as tiny subtitles. (This is the Polymarket-of-portfolio shot.)
2. **The "rolled-up" moment** — animated reveal where 7 fragmented USDC balances across 7 chains collapse into one row. The number that matters is "7 → 1."
3. **APY-on-idle** — the portfolio knows which of your assets are earning and which aren't. Hero data: weighted APY across the whole portfolio, or the dollar amount earning $0 (i.e., the cost of doing nothing).

## Sample "split" caption (only if this product has a two-state product motion)
fragmented vs rolled-up. before: 12 rows of USDC across 12 chains. after: one. that's portfolio.

## Voice notes
Portfolio is the most *emotional* product in the lineup — it shows people their actual financial reality. Voice has to be careful: never make people feel bad about a small balance, never make people feel smug about a big one. The wry register works best on the *experience of fragmentation* (the spreadsheet, the tabs, the mental math), not on the dollar amounts themselves. Confident tone on the consolidation tech; gentle tone on what users see when they open it.
