Brand voice fingerprints (from real corpora)

Infinex

  • Declarative leads: "X is live"
  • Single-noun openers used: "Tick, tock.", "INX is here."
  • Arrows → and ↓ as connective tissue
  • Sentence-case headers
  • Line breaks for visual rhythm (almost every announcement)
  • No ghost emoji, no hashtags, no "FYI"
  • Sign-off: → link, ↓, or nothing
  • Confident-flat, not consumer-fintech upbeat
Stock perps are now live on Infinex. Trade XYZ100 → top 100 US company index. 20x leverage. 24/7. Plus TSLA. NVDA. AAPL. GOOGL. HOOD. 19 markets total. Powered by @HyperliquidX and provided by @tradexyz.

Phantom

  • "FYI, X is now live..." opener
  • Title-Case-y, emoji-suffixed
  • Ghost emoji 👻 + product name as sign-off
  • Upbeat consumer-fintech voice
  • Three-bullet user-verb body
  • Replies in lowercase, shitposter-on-main
  • Compliance disclaimer as separate post
FYI, GameStop is now live for trading in Phantom 🎮

Hyperliquid

  • "The fact, alone" voice
  • Maximally terse, no adjectives
  • No emoji, no hashtags, no exclamation marks
  • Single-clause declaratives
  • Commanding-led, Practical-bodied
  • Sign-off: link, or none
$HYPE, the native token of the Hyperliquid L1, is live. Permissionless spot deploys are now live on mainnet.

Polymarket

  • News-anchor-neutral register
  • The market card IS the comms (visual carries weight)
  • Practical-led on product
  • Lets the numbers carry, doesn't punch
  • Third-party-as-author (journalists embed odds)
Polymarket is back in the US with sports and elections markets.

Findings from corpus comparison

  • Real Infinex opens with the FACT. Our generator opens with the THESIS. Every real Infinex announcement in the corpus starts with "X is now live" / "Y is here" / "Z is open." Our captions almost always start with a Sombre setup line ("The orderbook used to live somewhere else", "Every chain stood between a user and MegaETH"). This is the bigger voice gap than any docstring contamination — it's a structural mismatch with the actual Infinex announcement register.
  • Real Infinex announcements use specifics. Our captions stay abstract. IT017 lists 19 markets, leverage %, hours, dashboard location, two partner @-mentions. Our captions name maybe 1-2 deployed_facts at most. The generator is leaving deployed_facts on the table.
  • Real Infinex uses arrows as connective tissue and sign-off. → appears in IT008, IT020, IT024, IT029. ↓ in IT021, IT030. Our captions use line breaks but never arrows. Easy fix; not a voice problem, a format problem.
  • Real Infinex credits partners with @-mentions. IT017 ends "Powered by @HyperliquidX and provided by @tradexyz." IT030 says "powered by @HoudiniSwap 🔮". IT025 leads "Infinex 🤝 MegaETH". Our captions skip this entirely — partly because we didn't pass a partner field in the test card, but also because the generator doesn't reach for partner mentions.
  • None of our captions sound like Phantom, Hyperliquid, or Polymarket. Phantom would have "FYI" opener and ghost emoji. Hyperliquid would be one sentence with no embellishment. Polymarket would be news-anchor neutral. We don't drift toward any of those — we drift toward essayist, which is its own voice but not Infinex's announcement voice.
  • The Sombre-setup → Commanding-fact → Irradiant-lift beat pattern is consistent across all variants, but the real Infinex corpus shows that for announcements the Sombre prep is often skipped. IT017, IT020, IT025, IT030, IT031 all open directly with the Commanding fact. The 3-beat default we passed to the generator may be over-prescribed for announcement-class releases.

Implication: the docstring variant debate is largely cosmetic relative to two structural issues:

  1. The generator's default 3-beat shape — Sombre → Commanding → Irradiant — forces a philosophical opener that real Infinex announcements skip. Many real announcements are 1- or 2-beat (Commanding-only, or Commanding → Irradiant). The defaultBeatsForKind function probably needs per-kind defaults that match the actual register: data-card-official → Commanding-led + brief lift; launch-tier → 3-beat arc; maintenance → Self-Contained / Diffused only.
  2. The generator isn't using deployed_facts the way the real corpus uses them. Real Infinex announcements load the body with specifics; our captions stay abstract. This is downstream of the prompt — Stage B's drafting prompt likely needs a stronger "load the body with the deployed_facts that earn ship space" rule.

Both of these are bigger levers than the card.ts docstring choice. Worth flagging for the broader generator cleanup pass.
