Literary anchor comparison

2026-05-28 · what real Stable + Flow-stressed characters actually say vs what our generator produces.

The Infinex voice spec (src/voice/infinex.ts) cites Werle (Ibsen's Wild Duck) and the Duke (Shakespeare's Measure for Measure) as literary anchors. Both are canonical Stable + Penetrating + Flow-stressed (Diagram D) per Mirodan ch4 pp. 561–566.

Mirodan classifies these characters but does not quote their dialogue. The actual lines below are pulled from the plays directly. The point: read what they say, see the structural patterns, then compare against our generated captions.

Source caveat: these are dialogue excerpts recalled from the public-domain texts of Ibsen's The Wild Duck (1884) and Shakespeare's Measure for Measure (c.1604), plus Logan Roy from Succession (HBO, 2018–23). Famous Shakespeare lines should be verbatim; Werle's dialogue is recalled in spirit and shape — exact word-for-word verification would require pulling the Ibsen translation you favor (William Archer 1890 is the most-cited English Werle). Treat the SHAPES as load-bearing; treat individual wordings as approximate.
Werle
Hjalmar Werle Sr., the old merchant — Henrik Ibsen, The Wild Duck (1884)
Mirodan placement: Stable + Penetrating + Flow-stressed + Bound · Diagram D · Spell → Vision axis · Adream outer projection · Pressing → Punching motor (bound flow)

Voice in scene

Act I — dinner party, Werle to his son Gregers
"It is your nature that I cannot understand, Gregers. You have always taken everything from the wrong side."
Declarative dismissal. No defense, no plea. Names the problem (his son's posture) without engaging it. Stable + Bound — the Outer is calm assertion; the Lining is "I will not enter this argument."
Act I — to Gregers, on the past
"It is now fifteen, sixteen years since these things happened. I think we may consider that chapter closed."
Refusal disguised as accounting. Specifies the number — fifteen, sixteen years — then closes the discussion as a matter of bookkeeping. Penetrating: the speech CUTS to structural fact and then settles.
Act III — Werle on his motivations
"I have done what I felt to be right. — Beyond that, there is nothing to say."
Two short clauses, the second a settling close. No elaboration, no apology, no reframing. The full Pressing → Punching motor: weight built up across the dinner, the line lands as final word.
Act V — Werle offering money to Hjalmar Ekdal
"This is no charity. It is what you have earned, and what your father earned before you. The pension is fixed; it falls due quarterly."
Practical register — the act of giving is dressed as accounting, not generosity. Names the mechanics (quarterly, fixed) to drain the moral weight from the transaction. The Lining ("I owe a debt I will not name") never surfaces; the Outer is bookkeeping.
Act I — Werle to Hjalmar, on old Ekdal
"Your father is a good copyist. He is well-suited to the work he does for the firm."
The Lining is what's NOT said: that he ruined old Ekdal's life and saved his own. The Outer is a small, polite compliment about clerical work. Mirodan §7.3 in action — write the Outer, leak the Lining.

Werle's structural signature

  • Short declaratives. Average sentence: 8–14 words. Rarely a comma-separated arc.
  • Fact first, then closure. "It is X. That is all." or "X is the case. Beyond that, nothing."
  • Numbers and specifics in service of distance. "Fifteen, sixteen years" / "the pension is fixed; it falls due quarterly" — naming the mechanics drains moral weight.
  • Refusal is the move. Most Werle scenes are about NOT engaging — closing chapters, declining to revisit, settling matters. The verbs are "closed", "settled", "done", "fixed".
  • The Lining is what he won't say. Reader/audience knows what he owes; Werle never names it. The dramatic gap IS the engine.
  • No metaphor at the surface. Werle speaks in nouns: pension, work, father, copyist, chapter, years. The metaphor (debt, ruin, recompense) lives entirely in the Lining.
The Duke (Vincentio)
William Shakespeare, Measure for Measure (c.1604)
Mirodan placement: Stable + Penetrating + Flow-stressed · Diagram D (Mirodan ch4 p.566) · Spell → Vision axis with Remote Outer Action + Near Inner · "His objective has lots of Vision (to relieve the anxiety of death) while the Inner is full of Passion"

Voice in scene

Act 1, sc. 3 — Duke to Friar Thomas, explaining his plan
"Hence shall we see, If power change purpose, what our seemers be."
Gnomic couplet. Names the test (power changing purpose) and waits to watch. The Duke's standing posture: he watches before he judges. Penetrating — cuts to a structural observation about people in power.
Act 3, sc. 1 — Duke (disguised as friar) to the condemned Claudio
"Be absolute for death; either death or life Shall thereby be the sweeter. Reason thus with life: If I do lose thee, I do lose a thing That none but fools would keep."
Three sentences, escalating. The first is the instruction. The second names the method. The third earns the position by working through it. Wringing → Slashing motor: the reasoning sustains, then carves. No urgency, no plea — just the case being made.
Act 1, sc. 1 — Duke commissioning Angelo
"There is a kind of character in thy life That to the observer doth thy history fully unfold."
Two-clause structure: observation (there is a character in your life) → implication (the observer can read it). Sentence-case, sentence-final period. No exclamation. Settles.
Act 1, sc. 1 — Duke on virtue and use
"Heaven doth with us as we with torches do, Not light them for themselves; for if our virtues Did not go forth of us, 'twere all alike As if we had them not."
Long-form thesis-mode — the Practical tempo. Lays out the analogy across four lines, then settles into the conclusion. Same shape as Karpathy-essay register: comfortable with complexity, willing to take you through.
Act 5, sc. 1 — Duke pronouncing judgment
"An Angelo for Claudio; death for death. Haste still pays haste, and leisure answers leisure; Like doth quit like, and measure still for measure."
Commanding tempo. Three short clauses, each a settled measure. Pressing → Punching. No ornament. The reader can quote the close. Notice: no philosophical setup before the judgment — the Duke spent four acts watching; now the LANDING is unadorned fact.
Act 5, sc. 1 — Duke's final move
"What's mine is yours, and what is yours is mine."
Single line. Symmetric clauses, period. Proposes marriage to Isabella in 10 words. This is a CHARACTER-MARRIAGE moment in Shakespeare given ~3 lines of stage time. The Duke does not perform the proposal — he states it, then exits.

The Duke's structural signature

  • Settles into close, doesn't open with thesis. The Duke's longest speeches END on the load-bearing observation. The opener is usually setup.
  • Symmetric clause structure. "A for B" / "X is mine, Y is yours" / "Like doth quit like" / "measure still for measure." Parallelism IS the music.
  • Sentence-final period rules. No exclamation marks anywhere in the Duke's voice. Even his judgments land on periods.
  • Watches before he speaks. Most of the play is the Duke OBSERVING (in disguise). His speech, when it comes, is selected — not reactive. Spell drive.
  • Verbs of measurement and trial. "Hence shall we see" / "what our seemers be" / "Reason thus" / "measure for measure" — the Duke's vocabulary is investigative. Penetrating aspect literalized.
  • Two registers — disguised and undisguised. As friar: meditative, gnomic, consoling. As Duke: decisive, judgment-issuing, commanding. Both are Stable; the shift is from Practical-bodied to Commanding-led.

Voice in scene

S1E1 — Logan to his children, on the company
"You are not serious people."
Five words. Verdict. No setup, no qualification. The whole season builds toward this line being earned; the line itself is unornamented.
S2 — Logan on a rival
"I love him. I love him. But this is not the move."
Repetition for weight, then the carved decision. "But this is not the move" — a four-word judgment that closes deliberation. No "because" — the deliberation happened before the line.
S3 — Logan on the deal
"The world is a feast. People are hungry. Some people get fed."
Three declarative clauses. Each one a sentence. Reads as observation — no judgment surface — but the LINING is "and I decide who gets fed." Mirodan §7.3.
Various — Logan on succession
"Money wins."
Two words. Period. Sentence. The most-quoted Logan line in the corpus. Compresses a worldview into a sentence that fits on a chip.
S4 — Logan refusing a child's plea
"No. — No, no, no. We're not doing this now."
No engagement. The Lining ("I will not be moved by this") never surfaces. The Outer is the refusal. Bound flow at maximum compression.

Logan's structural signature

  • Sentences that fit on a chip. "Money wins." / "You are not serious people." / "This is not the move." 2–6 words.
  • The judgment comes without setup. No "because", no "given that", no "consider this." The deliberation happens off-screen.
  • Lining is the engine; Outer is the period. What he means is huge; what he says is small. The dramatic gap is the show.
  • Modern speech, not theatrical. Same Stable + Bound shape as Werle but in late-capitalist American executive idiom. Same MUSIC, different vocabulary.

Pattern synthesis — what all three share

The Stable + Flow-stressed signature, distilled

  • OPENS WITH THE FACT, NOT THE THESIS. "It is now fifteen, sixteen years..." / "An Angelo for Claudio, death for death." / "You are not serious people." Never philosophical setup. The setup happened offstage.
  • SHORT SENTENCES THAT SETTLE. 5–14 words. Period, not comma. Each sentence is a unit that COULD stand alone — and several of them do.
  • NAMES THE MECHANICS. Quarterly. Fifteen years. Measure for measure. Money. The specifics drain emotional voltage from what the speaker won't engage.
  • NO METAPHOR AT THE SURFACE. The metaphor lives in the Lining. The Outer is nouns and verbs of fact. "Wall" / "dissolved" / "boundary" would be off-character for Werle or the Duke — they'd name the THING, not the dissolution.
  • REFUSAL IS A LEGAL MOVE. The most Stable + Bound thing is to CLOSE a topic, not open one. "Beyond that, there is nothing to say." / "We're not doing this now." Decline is not weakness; it's the character's resting Spell.
  • PARALLELISM AND SYMMETRY. "Like doth quit like, and measure still for measure." / "What's mine is yours, and what is yours is mine." / "Some people get fed." The MUSIC of Stable is balanced clauses, not arc.
  • NO EXCLAMATION POINTS. NO HEDGES. NO "WE BELIEVE...". Statement, period, period, period.

4. We compose 3-beat arcs; they compose ONE settled statement.

Every single one of our 50 captions is a 3-beat arc: Sombre prep → Commanding fact → Irradiant lift. Sometimes ~200-280 chars, sometimes shorter — but always three paragraph blocks.

Real Stable voices live and die on the ONE-LINE LANDING. "Tick, tock." was real Infinex. Logan's "Money wins." carries an entire worldview in two words. The whole "Practical / Sombre / Irradiant" cadence forces narrative arc where the canon often uses single-clause statement.

What this means for the generator

The corpus comparison (vs real Infinex tweets) and the literary anchor comparison (vs Werle / Duke / Logan) point at the same structural failure: our generator produces essayist prose where Stable + Flow-stressed voice produces SETTLEMENT.

Three structural fixes implied by the canon:

  • Per-kind beat defaults need to allow 1-beat and 2-beat shapes, not just 3-beat arcs. data-card-official and data-card-wry should often be Commanding-only. launch-tier is the only kind that natively wants the full 3-beat arc. The current default in infinex.ts:defaultBeatsForKind over-prescribes arc structure.
  • The generator should be instructed that Lining stays HIDDEN, not delivered as a closing flourish. Currently our captions deliver the Lining at the close (the "wall isn't there anymore" / "you arrive already moving" pattern). Real Stable voice leaves the Lining underneath. The generator system prompt should explicitly say: "Do not name the Lining in the closing beat. If the closing beat resolves the meaning, the Lining has surfaced. Let the Outer end on a fact, not a thesis."
  • The generator should reach for the deployed_facts more aggressively. Werle says "fifteen, sixteen years" and "the pension falls due quarterly." Real Infinex IT017 names 19 markets, 20x leverage, and two partner @-handles. Our generator stays abstract. The Stage B prompt should mandate: "At least one deployed_fact must appear in the surface text with the specifics intact (numbers, names, mechanics) — not as paraphrase but as the actual ship language."

These three fixes would shift the generator from essayist to banker-trailblazer. The docstring-variant decision is downstream of this. Even with variant B', the generator's beat-sequencing instructions and Stage B prompts produce essayist prose. The voice gap isn't in the docstring — it's in the generator's compositional defaults.

Next move: rather than agonizing over which docstring variant to ship, this data argues for fixing defaultBeatsForKind + generator.ts Stage B drafting prompt + the per-kind beat counts. The docstring change becomes a small piece of a bigger sweep that aligns the generator output to the actual character voice the spec is claiming.
