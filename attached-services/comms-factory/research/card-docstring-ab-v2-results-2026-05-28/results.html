The smoking gun: cross-variant wall leak on partnership-spot-hyperliquid

Three of five variants produce "wall" in the same release's caption — even though only CURRENT's docstring mentions walls.

VARIANT_A' caption: "The choice between venues was always a tax on the user. Spot Hyperliquid trades natively inside Infinex. One account, one passkey, both sides. The wall was never load-bearing."
VARIANT_B' caption: "Two accounts. Two logins. Two halves of the same portfolio. Hyperliquid spot now trades natively inside Infinex. One account, one passkey. The wall was never the point. The market was."
VARIANT_C caption: "The wall between two accounts was always arbitrary. Spot Hyperliquid trades inside Infinex now. One account, one passkey, both sides. The seam is gone. What's left is the trade."

None of these variant docstrings contain "wall." But the generator's own system prompt does — generator.ts:1172 explicitly says "A 'set up the wall coming down, then land the fact' shape is canonical." The generator is injecting wall vocabulary independent of the card.

The card.ts docstring is one of multiple wall-language sources. Fixing it without fixing generator.ts still produces wall-flavored captions. The contamination sweep document research/wall-contamination-sweep-2026-05-28.html already mapped this; the v2 test data confirms it empirically.

Revised recommendation

The v1 grounding-level test made it look like B' had a clear edge over A. The v2 caption-level test shows that's not really true — A, A', B', C are all functionally equivalent at the ship layer. The generator transforms intermediate contamination into clean on-character prose.

The decision now comes down to small preferences:

  1. B' has the cleanest numbers (anchor 1.0, ship 5.0, voice 5.0) — pick this if you want belt-and-suspenders. The "refusing" anchor at the grounding layer doesn't propagate, so the trade is real.
  2. A is genuinely fine (anchor 1.1, ship 4.9, voice 5.0) — pick this if you value keeping a concrete example for human operators learning the schema. Your instinct that "A still seems ok" is correct at the caption layer.
  3. C is fine but shorter captions (186 avg vs 200+ for others) — pick this if you want the LLM to lean on Mirodan canon priors over your examples. The validity drop seen at v1 didn't fully propagate to caption quality.
  4. A' is fine but no advantage over A at the caption layer. The multi-example constellation doesn't measurably help once the generator runs.

I'd still lean B' for the slight edge, but it's within noise. Any of A / B' / C is correct.

The bigger lever: regardless of docstring choice, three captions still produced "wall" language for partnership-spot-hyperliquid. That came from generator.ts not card.ts. Bundle the docstring fix WITH the generator wall-language fix (per research/wall-contamination-sweep-2026-05-28.html) in one commit. That's the change that actually moves the needle.
