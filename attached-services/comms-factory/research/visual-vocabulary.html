01The new baseline

Four moves every brand in the set already makes. Not differentiators. The cost of admission.

  1. One accent on near-black

    Phantom #AB9FF2. Hyperliquid mint-teal ~#97FCE4. Polymarket #2E5CFF. Berachain honey-gold ~#F5B82E. Monad #6E54FF. Pendle is the exception — they run two semantic colors (#1BE3C2 PT-green = principal, #7AB7FF YT-blue = yield) — same discipline applied to a glossary instead of a single accent. Every saturated pixel earns its meaning.

  2. Bespoke or editorial-grade type

    Phantom commissioned F37 to cut a custom face. Hyperliquid runs a serif as display (rare in crypto). Pendle paid for Kyrial Sans Pro. Monad runs Britti Sans + Roboto Mono used decoratively. Polymarket is Inter-only — that's now the floor, not the ceiling. Plain Inter alone reads as default.

  3. Restrained motion

    No glitch, no CRT scanline, no Matrix rain, no rainbow conic gradient, no neon glow, no Tron grid, no five-color brand soup. Motion serves the message — type reveals, draw-in charts, mascot beats — never decoration.

  4. Product- and chain-agnostic discipline

    Phantom is the loudest on this: every announcement is purple, regardless of which chain is launching. Hyperliquid never breaks into "the perp color" or "the spot color." Infinex is multi-chain and multi-venue — refusing to color-code chains is the move; chain references live as text/logo labels inside Infinex's palette, not as accent swings.

03Template families to sketch first

What goes into Remotion before anything else. Build in this order. Note: per §00 workflow rule, these are infrastructure scaffolds; final content fills in once brand-factory locks Infinex's spec.

i.
Official register · highest leverage

The data card

Polymarket's gift to the category. Take a live Infinex data point — swap rate, bridge progress, portfolio delta, yield % — and treat it as the protagonist. Big-numeric in the display face, single accent color, one-line caption, small icon. Every video is a screenshot waiting to be re-shared. The product already produces the content; the template just frames it.

ii.
Cultural register · paired with i.

The data wry

Same data card, but the caption does the in-on-the-joke work. Same accent, same type, same chrome — different copy register. The chart stays sober; the joke lives in the caption. This is how Polymarket-style data-hero meets Monad-style two-register without bolting on a mascot.

iii.
Reserved · used ~4× per year

The launch tier

Berachain's lesson. A separate template that ONLY gets used for major events, with a reserved sub-palette and a reserved typographic move that signals "this one matters." Different accent within Infinex's system, different aspect ratio, different motion primitive. The scarcity is the signal.

iv.
Semantic · when there's a real split

The split

Pendle's lesson. If Infinex has any product motion that's semantically a split — in/out, bridge-from/bridge-to, principal/yield, spot/perp, on-chain/off-chain — build one template that visualizes the split with a reserved color pair. The split becomes Infinex's most legible visual primitive after the wordmark.

Defer (don't build yet): 3D mascot, character animation, illustrated explainer, scroll-cinema, anything that requires a serialized lore substrate Infinex doesn't have yet.
