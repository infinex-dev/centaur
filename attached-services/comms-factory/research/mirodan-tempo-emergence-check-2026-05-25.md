## Q1 — Does the actor CHOOSE tempo, or does it EMERGE from inner work?

**Verdict: Tempo EMERGES. It is not chosen. It is the visible/audible trace of subconscious Deciding modulating the other Inner Participations.**

The settling passage is on **p. 286 (Ch.1)**:

> "Inner Participations as a whole are revealed physically in the overall tempo of the character's movement. Tempo itself, being a function of Time, is the physical realization of the Inner Participation of Deciding. Subconscious Deciding is the basis upon which all other Inner Participations express themselves 'in time', reflecting the role of *primum mobile* played by Deciding in the order of the Inner Participations." (Mirodan, Ch.1 p. 286)

Three things are load-bearing here:
1. Tempo is **revealed**, not constructed — i.e. observed downstream of the inner machinery, not assigned upstream.
2. The agency is **Deciding**, not the actor's conscious will. And Deciding is **Subconscious** — not consciously volitional.
3. Tempo is what *all four* Inner Participations look like when they express "in time" — it is a system-level property of the inner cocktail, not a single dial the actor can spin.

The transitive-verb passage on **pp. 350-353 (Ch.1)** confirms the same point from the other direction — what the actor actually *picks* is not tempo, but a transitive verb:

> "Working Actions are abstractions; what an actor PLAYS is a transitive verb that *fits* a Working Action. 'Punching' can't be acted. 'To box' can." (Ch.1 p. 351)

Each transitive verb carries a "definite tempo" built into it (pp. 351-352). So even when the actor *selects* something, what they select is a verb that fits the Working Action that emerged from the Subconscious Motif. They never operate on tempo as a free dimension.

## Q2 — If emergent, from WHAT exactly does it emerge? Trace the chain.

**The chain in Mirodan's vocabulary, full citation:**

The deepest source is Malmgren's complete sequence (Ch.3 p. 529):

> `PSYCHOLOGICAL TYPE → SUPER-OBJECTIVE → EXTERNALIZED DRIVES (INNER) → INNER ATTITUDE → EXTERNALIZED DRIVES (OUTER) → ACTION ATTITUDES`

Layered onto that, per beat, is the **organic order** Malmgren uses for live generation (Ch.1 pp. 267-281 and the Adapting-first passage at p. 326):

1. **Mental Factors** (Sensing / Thinking / Intuiting / Feeling) sit **latent** until catalysed (Ch.1 p. 282: "Mental Factors are PASSIVE / LATENT. Only activated by external catalyst into Inner Participations").
2. **The external catalyst** = engagement with the **Objective** — i.e. the Stanislavskian Super-Objective + Given Circumstances + Obstacle. (Ch.1 p. 282; Super-Objective explicitly anchored at p. 306: "All fundamental motivations (Super-Objectives), says Yat Malmgren, are ultimately emotional in nature and therefore emerge from the Mental Factor of Feeling.")
3. **Adapting (Feeling)** is the first response to disturbance (Ch.1 p. 326): "Yat Malmgren considers that Characters react to reality through Flow first. Adapting is the first response to disturbance — characters thrown off balance by impact of others and adapt to restore balance."
4. **Sensing (Intending)** registers weight/material of the situation.
5. **Intuiting (Deciding)** — the *primum mobile* — **subconsciously selects** which of the other three Inner Participations to engage with the Objective:

   > "Deciding is the catalyst which allows the other three Inner Participations to function and reveal themselves. Unlike the other three Inner Participations, Deciding cannot manifest itself on its own. It needs to combine with one of the others to become manifest." (Ch.1 p. 302)

   > "Character first decides intuitively *which* function to engage with the Objective. Deciding precedes the others (p. 284). Hence 'primum mobile'." (Ch.1 p. 284)

6. **Thinking (Attending)** directs (where, on which trajectory).
7. The 3-axis cocktail of Inner Participations cocktail-resolves into the **Subconscious Motif** (Ch.1 p. 349 — the inner counterpart of a Working Action; same eight names — Pressing/Punching/Wringing/Slashing/Dabbing/Gliding/Flicking/Floating).
8. The Subconscious Motif generates *both* the outer **Working Action** (volitional, transitive-verb-playable) AND the **Shadow Moves** (involuntary 2-axis fragments leaking the Lining).
9. **The Working Action and its transitive-verb realisation have a definite intrinsic tempo** (Ch.1 pp. 351-352).

So the answer to "from WHAT does tempo emerge" is: **tempo is the audible/visible signature of step 9, which is locked-in once steps 1-8 have correctly run.** Tempo is *Deciding's expression in Time*, modulating the other Inner Participations. It is not produced *by* Deciding alone; Deciding is the *catalyst* that activates the others, and the resulting whole-character motion in time IS the tempo (Ch.1 p. 286).

**Spell-as-tempo confirmation (Ch.3 p. 537):** Drives have intrinsic tempo signatures too — Spell characters "generally display a Sustained tempo. Because Deciding is rhythmical/time-based, and Deciding is subdued in Spell, the resulting quality is 'dream-like: almost, but not literally, in slow motion'." This is a *derivation* from the Drive composition (Spell = Doing + Passion + Vision, Deciding subdued), not an actor choice.

**Lining is part of the chain too.** Mirodan, Ch.1 p. 357: the Outer Action gives "lip service" to the conscious wish; the Shadow Moves (driven by the Lining / hidden Subconscious Motif) leak the secret. So the chain that produces *visible tempo* runs through both the Outer Action *and* its Lining — the Lining colours the surface tempo by making Shadow Moves run at the Lining's tempo while the Working Action runs at the surface tempo. Two tempi can co-exist on one body (Ch.1 pp. 323-324: dynamic / intellectual / emotional tempo can differ — Orson Welles fast-sensing/slow-emotion; Garbo the inverse).

## Q3 — Is there ANY sense in which the actor "predisposes" toward certain tempi?

**Verdict: Yes — strongly. The actor's tempo space is *deeply constrained* upstream, but they have no direct lever on tempo itself.** The constraints are:

### Hard constraints (the actor cannot violate them):

1. **Inner Attitude is fixed per character** (Ch.2 p. 378: "real people... possess all the Inner Attitudes... Theatre characters, by contrast, have **fixed** Inner Attitudes"). A Stable character cannot enter Mobile. Period.
2. **Aspect is fixed per character** (Ch.2 p. 374). Enclosing vs Penetrating is a placement, not a setting.
3. **Preparation hierarchy is non-skippable** (Ch.1 p. 347): "We cannot carry out a Punching action without first undergoing the inner preparation of Pressing... This order cannot be reversed." Skipping the prep degrades the action — a Punch without Pressing becomes a Press, a Slash without Wringing becomes a Wring, a Dab without Gliding becomes a Glide, a Flick without Floating becomes a Float. So the *Sustained* prep tempo is structurally locked-in before any *Quick* execution tempo.
4. **Drive composition determines tempo signature** (Ch.3 pp. 537 — Spell = Sustained; Ch.3 p. 532 — Doing = the "dynamic Drive par excellence", tank/bulldozer). The character's confluence of Drives constrains the tempo space.
5. **Real flow is mutually exclusive with industrial flow in Working Actions** (Ch.1 p. 340) — only 3 of 4 Motion Factors are present in any Working Action; this is structural, not aesthetic.

### Soft constraints (within-character variability):

6. **Three of four Variations are available** within an Inner Attitude (Ch.3 p. 558): "a Character has at its disposal all four Variations given to its Inner Attitude. In practice, we find that one of these variations tends to occur less often, reflecting the character's Aspect." So *three* of the four tempo-Variations are live; one is suppressed by Aspect.
7. **Both Stresses are available**, dominant one fires most (Ch.2 p. 470): "Characters can switch between them within scenes, to create different relationships and different 'beats'."
8. **Stress can flip mid-scene** ("the swing of the pendulum" — Ch.2 pp. 470-473, Lear).

### What the actor's conscious lever actually is:

The actor's volitional surface is **the transitive verb** (Ch.1 pp. 350-352). They choose "to drill," "to nibble," "to gouge," "to patronise," "to chastise." Each verb fits a Working Action with intrinsic tempo. Verb selection constrains tempo *via* selecting the Working Action — but the verb itself is constrained by what the inner work (Adapting → Sensing → Deciding → Thinking) made available.

**So the proper formulation is:** the actor predisposes toward certain tempi by making **upstream commitments** (Super-Objective, Through-Action, Obstacle, Inner Attitude, Aspect, Stress, Drive confluence, Lining). These commitments *deterministically constrain* the tempo space the character can occupy. But within that space, tempo at any given moment is still *emergent* — it is what the resolved Subconscious Motif produces in time. The actor cannot reach in and assign "Sustained at beat 7."

## Q4 — Is the operator's exact memory ("Tempo is emergent from Deciding, never assigned") confirmed or imprecise?

**Verdict: Imprecise — directionally correct, but compresses two things and slightly misnames the source.**

### What's correct in the memory:
- "Never assigned" — **confirmed**. The actor never picks a tempo as a free dimension. The closest they come is selecting a transitive verb, which has tempo intrinsic to it.
- "Emergent" — **confirmed**. Mirodan's word is "revealed" (p. 286), but "emergent" captures the same downstream-of-the-inner-work mechanic.

### What's imprecise:
- "Emergent from Deciding" — this overstates Deciding's role. Tempo is the **physical realisation of Deciding** (Ch.1 p. 286), but Deciding is *primum mobile* — it does not produce tempo on its own. Mirodan, Ch.1 p. 302: **"Unlike the other three Inner Participations, Deciding cannot manifest itself on its own. It needs to combine with one of the others to become manifest."** So tempo is more accurately *emergent from the full Subconscious Motif (Inner Participations cocktail), with Deciding as the catalyst/primum mobile that allows the other three to express in time*.

### More accurate phrasing:

> "Tempo is **emergent from the resolved Subconscious Motif**, which is the 3-axis Inner Participation cocktail catalysed by Deciding (*primum mobile*) once the upstream chain (Super-Objective → Drives → Inner Attitude → Aspect+Stress → Lining → Adapting-first → Subconscious Motif) has run. Tempo is the audible/visible **trace** of Deciding modulating the other Inner Participations — never an independent dial the actor assigns."

The two-line summary the operator should keep in memory:

> **Tempo is never assigned. It is the trace of Deciding (*primum mobile*) modulating the other Inner Participations once the upstream inner work has resolved into a Subconscious Motif.**

This preserves the operator's working intuition while fixing the "from Deciding" causality slip — Deciding catalyses, the cocktail produces, the tempo reveals.

## Q5 — Practical implication for an LLM generator emitting `tempo_primary` per beat

### The strict reading (pure Mirodan-faithfulness):

Emitting `tempo_primary` per beat as an explicit field at the *generator* layer is a **Mirodan violation** *if* the generator is also the layer that chooses it freely from a closed set without upstream constraint. That's the Method-actor "cross to pour a drink with no functional connection" failure mode Mirodan warns against (Ch.1 p. 271-272).

### The viable reading (engineering-faithful to the framework):

`tempo_primary` per beat is **acceptable** if and only if:

1. **It is *derived* from upstream fields, not freely chosen.** The pipeline must compute tempo from the prior commitments: locked Inner Attitude (Stable) + Aspect (Penetrating) + Stress (Flow) + Drive composition (Spell-Vision per the locked Infinex placement) + the beat's Super-Objective / Through-Action / Obstacle / Lining. If `tempo_primary` is *output by* the generator with no upstream cause, the audience nervous system reads it as inconsistent (Ch.1 p. 356 Carpenter — "audience perception is quick to recognize an inconsistent movement").

2. **The tempo space per beat is *constrained* to the 3-of-4 Variations the locked Inner Attitude permits, biased toward the dominant Stress, with the suppressed Variation downweighted.** Free-floating tempo selection violates the framework; constrained-derivation respects it.

3. **The preparation hierarchy is enforced across consecutive beats.** A Quick beat must be preceded (in the same post or the previous one) by its Sustained partner. Punching needs a Pressing prep; Slashing needs a Wringing prep; Dabbing needs a Gliding prep; Flicking needs a Floating prep (Ch.1 p. 347). A generator that emits `tempo_primary: "Punch"` without an adjacent `tempo_primary: "Press"` prep is structurally degraded — the Punch will actually read as a Press anyway (the framework's own self-correction).

4. **What the generator should output as its *conscious* layer is not the tempo but the equivalent of the *transitive verb*** — the concrete "to scold," "to soothe," "to drill," "to glide" verbs that fit the Working Action. Tempo is then *read off* the verb. Emitting `tempo_primary` as a label on a beat is fine for the validator / classifier / observer; it is dubious as a *generator output* unless explicitly framed as derived.

### Concrete recommendation for the comms-factory generator:

- **Inputs per beat** (load-bearing, must be present upstream): Super-Objective for this release, Through-Action for the post, Obstacle the post negotiates, Lining (the hidden contradiction the surface text covers), the locked Infinex placement (Inner Attitude + Aspect + Stress + Drive confluence).
- **Generator's job**: pick **transitive verbs / Working-Action-fitting phrases** that resolve the inner cocktail, respecting preparation hierarchy.
- **`tempo_primary` per beat**: keep it if useful for the validator / Laban classifier, but **mark it `derived: true`** and compute it from the verb + the Working Action it fits. Do **not** let the generator choose `tempo_primary` directly as a free field with no upstream constraint.
- **Validator check**: any beat where the chosen verb's intrinsic Working Action doesn't fit the locked placement's 3-of-4 Variation set should fail. Any Quick beat without a preceding Sustained prep should fail (or downgrade to its Sustained equivalent automatically).

This is the engineering reading that respects both Mirodan's actor-process discipline AND the practical need for explicit fields in a typed pipeline. The operator's instinct ("tempo is emergent, never assigned") translates to: **derived field, with upstream constraints checked, not free output**.
