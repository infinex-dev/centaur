## Claim-by-claim audit

### Claim 1 — Placement = Stable + Penetrating + Flow-stress (bound pole), Spell→Vision, Diagram D
**Verdict:** ✓ Canonical
- Ch4 p. 566 (Duke applied example): Stable, Flow-stressed, with Spell-Vision Action axis and Diagram D structure (penetrating implicit via "Attending emphasized" per Ch2 p. 199).
- Ch3 p. 557 confirms 24 diagrams = 6 attitudes × 4 (stress × aspect).
- Arrow notation "Spell→Vision" canonical per Ch3 p. 183 footnote: `→ = cause/effect from Character to Action`.

### Claim 2 — Five main tempi assignments + motor pairs
**Verdict:** ✓ Canonical
- Ch2 p. 494 (Stable Variations): Strong/Direct = Commanding (Punching/Pressing); Strong/Flexible = Practical (Wringing/Slashing).
- Ch2 p. 453 (Adream Variations): Strong/Bound = Sombre; Light/Free = Irradiant. Ch2 p. 461 explicit ("Pressing more introvert. Lear's curse..."). Ch2 p. 462 explicit ("Irradiant unfolding or irradiant enfolding...").
- Ch2 p. 432 + 447 (Remote Variations): Direct/Free = Sociable; "REMOTE DIRECT/FREE ('sociable')" explicit canonical label.

### Claim 3 — Plain-English register labels (Future-warmth / Institutional drop / Weight-of-conviction / Ecosystem warmth / Long-form thesis)
**Verdict:** ~ Honest plain-English translation, within tolerance
- *Institutional drop* (Commanding): aligns precisely with Mirodan's *"Commanding demonstration of bold resolve"* (Ch2 p. 494). ✓
- *Future-warmth* (Irradiant): loose translation of Mirodan's *"irradiant unfolding/enfolding of sympathetic exultation"* (Ch2 p. 462). ~
- *Weight-of-conviction* (Sombre): captures emotional heft but omits prep/bound mechanics. Mirodan's canonical adjectives center on *gravity / body-blow physicality / Pressing prep* (Ch2 p. 461 — Lear's curse). ~
- *Ecosystem warmth* (Sociable): fair English for Mirodan's *"developing or contracting feeling of sociable companionship"* (Ch2 p. 447). ~
- *Long-form thesis* (Practical): interpretive — Mirodan calls Practical "developing intention to cast/submit to spell-binding power" (Ch2 p. 495). The sheet's "long-form thesis" is a CHARACTER APPLICATION, not a literal Mirodan adjective. ~

**Drift severity:** LOW. These are operator-coined plain English for voice-work use, not direct Malmgren vocabulary. The glossary should note this explicitly.

**Fix applied:** glossary preamble now flags the labels as "operator-coined plain English, not direct Mirodan vocabulary."

- Rule 1 distinction in the sheet maps correctly.

### Claim 6 — Passion is hidden lining (X-diagram top-left), off-spec when surfacing as extravert
**Verdict:** ✓ Canonical

- For Stable with Spell/Doing formative (Diagram D), the action-axis drives are Doing→Passion (top corners). Ch3 p. 188.
- Passion surfaces under stress but is NOT the baseline Character Drive. The sheet's claim is exact.

### Claim 7 — Beat sequence mechanism: Sombre (Pressing-bound) preps Commanding (Pressing-free)

- The sheet's claim that Sombre is the *bound* version and Commanding is the *free* version is precise.

### Claim 8 — Literary anchors: Werle (Wild Duck) + Duke (Measure for Measure) for Stable+Penetrating+Flow Diagram D

### Claim 9 — 5 main + 7 beat-only = 12 of 24 tempi; "the other 12 belong to other brands"
**Verdict:** ✗ Conceptually loose — conflated Variations with Diagrams
- Ch3 p. 201: 24 diagrams = 6 Inner Attitudes × 4 (Stress × Aspect) sub-diagrams per attitude. Each diagram contains 4 Variations (the "tempi" at that level).
- Ch2 p. 470: *"most characters use at least three of the four 'tempi' open to them at the level of the Inner Attitude."* A Stable character has access to all 4 Stable Variations.
- The sheet's "12 of 24 tempi" framing conflates the Variation lattice (4 per attitude) with the Diagram grid (24 total).
- The accurate structure for Infinex (Stable + Penetrating + Flow-stress):
  - 4 Stable Variations (Commanding, Practical, Receptive, Self-Contained) — always accessible
  - Outer-action projections via Flow-stress: to Adream (Sombre, Irradiant, Overpowering, Diffused) and Remote (Egocentric, Altruistic, Sociable, Unsociable) — accessible via the stress mechanic
  - The "other 12" (4 Near Variations + 4 Mobile outer-actions + 4 Awake outer-actions) are structurally accessed by *Time-stressed* Stable (ProjectJin's territory) and Near-baseline characters (Cream-adjacent) — not "off-spec" in absolute terms, but outside Infinex's Flow-stress mechanics.

**Drift severity:** MEDIUM. The voice spec itself is sound; the structural justification was loose. The reframe ties back to ProjectJin properly.

**Fix applied:** "rare accents" section in the sheet now explains the access mechanic correctly — Infinex's Flow-stress projects through Adream + Remote; ProjectJin's Time-stress would project through Near + Awake instead. Same brand family, different stress mechanics.

### Claim 10 — Stable-D archetype: "watching from rest" (Mirodan) vs "banker-turned-crypto trailblazer / forward-motion" (operator)
**Verdict:** ~ Tension is acknowledged and resolved in the sheet
- Ch2 p. 488: Stable is *"like a powerful car... always giving the impression of knowing exactly where it is going."* Forward-motion, not reactive.
- Ch2 p. 489: Stable characters *"appear in control of events... elevated social or spiritual status... all important decisions have already been taken."*
- Flow-stressed Stable (Duke) is *"softer, more sophisticated"* (Ch2 p. 263) — allows inward reflection — but still moves via pre-taken decisions.
- The operator's "banker-turned-crypto trailblazer" is forward + decisions-already-taken: a startup adaptation of the Mirodan archetype that preserves the grammar (Spell→Vision, patient + future-pointing + non-urgent + held-back-power) while updating the literal "watching from disguise" passivity to active mapping. Not drift — intentional adaptation.

**Drift severity:** NONE. Tension is genuine and resolved.
