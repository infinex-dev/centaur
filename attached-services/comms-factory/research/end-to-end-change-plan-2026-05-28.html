End-to-end change plan

2026-05-28 · Laban/Mirodan-canonical, citation-anchored, structurally repetition-resistant. Every prescription either cites canonical source or is a forbidden-list — never substitute language that becomes the next anchor.

Three architectural rules

  1. Canon-by-citation, not by paraphrase. Every framework-layer instruction that prescribes voice/movement principles must cite the source (Mirodan vol/page, Stanislavski text, or a public-domain literary anchor like Wild Duck Act V or Measure for Measure Act 5). No operator restatement. If the LLM needs the principle, it gets the canonical formulation, not our gloss.
  2. Forbidden-lists, not example-lists. Examples become templates (verified across A/B/C/D/E test — every example, even token-swapped, drives 33-50% of outputs toward its frame). Use NEGATIVE prescription ("do not X") plus canonical reference, NOT positive prescription ("do Y like this example"). Variant C's architecture extended pipeline-wide.
  3. Beat counts emerge from the verb, not from defaults. Per Mirodan §1.7 (vol 2, p. 347): tempo is read from how the verb is played. Pre-prescribing 3-beat arcs forces structure where canon would compose 1 or 2 beats. defaultBeatsForKind must allow shorter shapes — the Actor's verb selection determines beat count.

Anti-anchoring discipline summary

  1. Citations over examples. When the LLM needs a shape, give it the canonical source (Mirodan §X.Y vol Z p. AA; Wild Duck Act V; Measure for Measure 5.1; Infinex IT017). The LLM has the source in training; the citation activates the prior. An example becomes a template.
  2. Forbidden-lists over example-lists. Where positive prescription is necessary, frame as "must contain X from card.deployed_facts" — making the positive constraint card-specific rather than pipeline-fixed. The form varies per card; the rule is constant.
  3. Multiple literary references when one example would anchor. Werle AND the Duke AND Logan Roy — three references that share the placement but use different vocabularies. The LLM has to integrate the shape across instances, no single instance to template.
  4. Beat counts emerge, not prescribed. Per-kind defaults are STARTING POINTS the Actor can compress. Stage A Inner-Work prompt should explicitly permit single-beat compositions when the verb is self-contained.
  5. No operator-coined terminology in framework code. "Subconscious Motif" (gone), "wall coming down" (gone), "dissolved-wall thesis" (gone), "refusing to perform the anti-pattern" (gone). Replace with Mirodan-canonical labels or kill the field.
