# Literary-anchor drift escalation policy

**Status:** diagnostic playbook, not a production gate.

Run a literary-anchor comparison only when outputs drift into one of these patterns:

- generic AI/product-launch copy
- over-Commanding decrees
- repeated wall/map/territory image systems
- Passion/urgency/FOMO surfaces
- final copy that performs marketing instead of settled authority

For Infinex, the diagnostic anchor is Stable + Penetrating + Flow, Diagram D:

- Werle from *The Wild Duck*
- the Duke from *Measure for Measure*

Use anchors to ask one question:

> Does this read like settled authority with hidden pressure, or like marketing performance?

Do not put literary quotes or examples into generator prompts as reusable copy templates. If an anchor excerpt appears in analysis, mark it diagnostic-only and do not feed it back as final-copy vocabulary.
