## Cross-post synthesis

### The recurring template (15/15 posts use most of it)

```
[Top nav]
[H1 — tagline-shaped headline, often "Introducing X" or "X is the best place to..."]
[Subheadline — single line of value prop OR rhetorical Q+A]
[Byline — Author Name • Date]
[Category tags — 2-5 tags from {Beginner|Intermediate} × {Read|Watch} × {topic}]
[Hero image — always static, never animated]
[Table of contents — anchor-linked H2 list]

[H2 #1 — feature/concept]
  paragraph + (optional) bulleted list + (optional) screenshot

[H2 #2 — feature/concept]
  paragraph + (optional) bulleted list + (optional) screenshot

[... 3-7 H2s total ...]

[H2 final — almost always "Looking ahead" or "What's coming next"]
  forward-looking close, no hard CTA

[Inline CTA — "Open the Phantom app and tap X" OR waitlist link]
[Legal/disclaimer block — if regulated product (perps, debit card, etc.)]

[Newsletter signup]
[Related articles — 2-5 cards with thumbnails]
[Footer nav: Product / Resources / Company / Socials]
```

### Length norms

- **Acquisition/corp news:** ~380 words, no TOC, prose-only.
- **Funding/strategic:** ~650-700 words, no TOC (read-through), one pull quote, one metric list.
- **Feature launch (small):** 550-900 words, 3-5 H2s, 1-3 screenshots, TOC.
- **Major product launch:** 800-1,200 words, 4-7 H2s, 1-2 images, TOC, often a "Looking ahead" close.
- **Chain integration:** ~800 words, 5 H2s, ecosystem diagram, TOC, steps as numbered prose (NOT code blocks).
- **Older posts (pre-2024 H2 2025):** longer (1,100-1,650), more H3 nesting, sometimes FAQ, sometimes embedded video. **All trimmed in newer posts.**

The 2024-2026 drift is unmistakable: Phantom **compressed** their template. Shorter posts, fewer subheadings, no FAQ, no video embeds, no how-to step density. The current shape is leaner — 4-6 H2s, prose-with-occasional-list, screenshots where helpful.

### Heading shape (recurring patterns)

H2 headings cluster around four shapes:

1. **Verb-noun feature label** — "Spend anywhere with your Phantom Debit Card", "Modify position size with ease", "Track your most important data points"
2. **Concept explainer (question form)** — "What is liquid staking?", "Why use perps?", "Why trade perps in Phantom?"
3. **How-to** — "Getting started", "How to get started", "How it works"
4. **Forward-looking close** — "Looking ahead", "Looking forward", "Looking forward to what's next", "What's coming next"

15/15 posts end on a forward-looking H2 OR a "Looking ahead"-style paragraph. **This is the most reliable structural signature in Phantom's blog.**

### What's IN every post (the mandatory spine)

- H1 + subheadline + byline (author name, no "by", with date)
- 2-5 category tags chosen from a controlled vocabulary: difficulty (Beginner/Intermediate), format (Read/Watch), topic (Solana/DeFi/Cash/News/Security/etc.)
- 1 hero image (always static)
- Newsletter signup before footer
- Footer with Product/Resources/Company/Socials columns
- Related-articles row (except shortest news posts)

### What's OPTIONAL

- TOC (~13/15 posts — skipped only on short news/funding)
- Pull quote (~2/15 — used when the speaker carries weight: CEO, General Counsel)
- Bulleted lists (~12/15)
- Inline screenshots/images beyond hero (~10/15)
- Legal disclaimer block (only on regulated products: perps, debit, derivatives)
- CTA button (often absent; usually it's an inline link to "open the app" or "join the waitlist")

### Clanker-readable affordances — present, but PARTIAL

What Phantom does that helps LLMs:
- **Clean H1/H2/H3 hierarchy** (no decorative subheadings, no all-caps, no emoji headings).
- **Stable byline+date metadata** in a predictable position (under H1).
- **Tag vocabulary is controlled** (small set, reused).
- **TOC = explicit anchor list** of section IDs.
- **URLs are slug-cased and semantic** (`/introducing-phantom-cash`, not `/post/2026-09-30-2814`).
- **Related-articles row** is structured (title + thumbnail + link), parseable.

What Phantom does NOT do (and Infinex should add):
- **No TL;DR / summary block at top.** Every post requires reading the lede + first H2 to understand the announcement. Bad for agents on a budget.
- **No FAQ section** (deprecated after the 2023 crosschain post). Q&A is the most agent-friendly shape; Phantom dropped it.
- **No code blocks ever.** Including in `/anti-spoofing-security` which is a technical post. Setup steps are written as prose ("Open the app, tap Settings, then..."). Agents have to parse step semantics from prose.
- **No structured fact box.** Yields, supported chains, fee schedules, region availability — all live inline in prose, with disclaimers in footers. An agent answering "what regions is Phantom Cash available in" has to read the whole legal disclaimer.
- **No semantic JSON-LD / schema.org markup** evident (would need DOM inspection to confirm but the rendered output has no signals of it).
- **No anchor links inside H2s** (other than the TOC). Sub-claims can't be deep-linked.

### Image/motion treatment — sharply different from Phantom's Twitter

Phantom on Twitter: motion-rich, GIF-heavy, kinetic typography, character animations. **Phantom on blog: static images only.** Across 15 posts:
- ~0 GIFs.
- ~0 inline videos in the modern posts (`/cross-chain-swapper` 2023 had one embed; gone in newer posts).
- 1-5 static images per post, usually 1-3.
- Screenshots are flat product UI, no annotation arrows, no callouts.

**Hypothesis:** the blog is the "stable" surface; Twitter is the "kinetic" surface. The blog reads as documentation that motion-rich tweets link INTO. The motion treatment is reserved for upstream of the funnel; the blog post is the rest stop with no animation.

This is structurally important for Infinex: the blog is the **anchor doc**, the tweet is the **trailer**. Don't mix them.

### What's INCONSISTENT (don't copy without thinking)

- **Yield/risk disclaimers** are missing on the PSOL post but heavy on the perps post. Phantom is inconsistent on financial disclosures. Infinex (banker-shaped, Penetrating Effort) should be MORE consistent here, not less.
- **Author choice** varies by purpose. Product launches → product PM. Strategic moves → CEO. Regulatory → General Counsel. Security → engineer. Acquisitions → CEO. This is deliberate and good — copy it.
- **Hero image style** has drifted over time (older posts had illustrated/character-led hero art; newer posts trend toward product screenshots + soft branded backgrounds). Indicates ongoing visual system iteration; treat as observation, not target.

## What Infinex should STEAL (structure)

1. **The forward-looking H2 close.** "Looking ahead" / "What's coming next" — 15/15 posts. Cheap signal of momentum without making concrete promises. Infinex-coded too: future-tense, Stable Time, craft-patience. Adopt verbatim.
2. **Byline + role.** "Author Name, Role • Date". Lets the reader weight the post. Use the same Author→Role mapping logic: PM for products, CTO for tech, founder for strategy, GC for regulatory.
3. **Controlled tag vocabulary.** Difficulty × Format × Topic — three orthogonal axes, small sets. Don't sprawl.
4. **Slug-cased semantic URLs.** `/hyperliquid-spark`, not `/blog/posts/2026/05/launch-hl-spark-v1`.
5. **TOC under byline for any post >600 words.** Anchor links to each H2. Skip only on <500-word news.
6. **H2 shapes — pick from the 4 patterns:** verb-noun feature / question explainer / how-to / forward-looking. Don't invent fifth-shape headings.
7. **Length norms per kind:**
   - Acquisition/news: ~400 words, prose-only.
   - Funding/strategy: ~700 words.
   - Feature launch: 600-900 words.
   - Major product launch: 800-1,200 words.
   - Chain integration: ~800 words.
8. **Static-only images in blog.** Save motion for Twitter/X. Blog is the anchor doc, not the trailer.
9. **Newsletter + related articles before footer.** Both consistently present; both cheap to wire.
10. **Inline CTA over button.** "Open the Phantom app and tap Cash" reads as instruction, not marketing. Banker-trailblazer-coded.

## What Infinex should ADD (Phantom-shaped gaps to fill)

1. **TL;DR block above the TOC.** 3-5 bullets, agent-readable, fact-dense. Phantom doesn't do this; Infinex should. Clanker-readability is a stated goal.
2. **FAQ section at the bottom** of any post about a financial product. Phantom dropped FAQ in 2024; Infinex should re-add for products with real user questions (Spark yields, Synth liquidations, bridge times).
3. **Code blocks for any setup that involves a config value, an address, or a slippage tolerance.** Phantom writes prose ("set slippage to 0.5%"); Infinex should put `0.5%` in a code block. Agents grep for code blocks.
4. **Structured fact box** at top: chains supported, fee, region availability, audit links. One per launch post. Agents parse boxes.
5. **JSON-LD / schema.org `Article` markup** in `<head>` for every post — give agents an explicit type signal. Cheap, mostly invisible to humans, very visible to crawlers/agents.
6. **Anchor IDs on every H2/H3** automatically. Phantom does this for TOC only; do it for every heading so any claim is deep-linkable from a tweet or another post.
7. **Risk disclosure block, consistent shape**, on every yield/perps/synthetics post. Banker-shaped consistency. Don't be Phantom-inconsistent here.

## What Infinex should AVOID

- **Don't mimic Phantom's warmer/Sociable openers** — "We're thrilled to announce", "Wen X? Right now." Infinex is Stable + Penetrating + Spell-Vision. Opening line should land like a clean fact, not a hype curtain-raiser. The structural shell is transferable; the tonal lead-in is not.
- **Don't mimic the all-prose technical posts.** `/anti-spoofing-security` is structurally weak for the topic — zero code blocks for a security explainer is a Phantom-specific choice that doesn't serve readers. Infinex audience (CTO-coded ICs reading docs alongside agents) needs the code.
- **Don't drop FAQ sections** the way Phantom did. They removed them; the rationale isn't clear and the affordance is high-value for agents.
- **Don't sprawl headings.** Older Phantom posts had H2+H3 nesting (`/bitcoin-phantom` ran 8 levels deep); newer ones cap at 4-6 H2s. Match the newer cap.
- **Don't import Phantom's tag soup.** Their tags work for them; Infinex needs its own vocabulary (e.g., Difficulty × Format × {Spark, Synth, Bridge, Cash, Custody, Markets, Security, Company}). Pick before launch, don't iterate later.
- **Don't put motion in the blog.** Phantom keeps blog static; Infinex should too. Motion lives in tweets/launch pages. Mixing surfaces confuses the system.

## Minimum pattern extracted

Strip every optional. Ten-line skeleton:

```
1. H1 — fact-shaped headline (verb-noun product label OR "Introducing X")
2. Subheadline — one line of value-prop
3. Byline — Author Name, Role • YYYY-MM-DD
4. Hero image — static
5. TOC — anchor links to each H2 (skip if <500 words)
6. H2 #1 — concept explainer ("What is X?" / "Why X?")
7. H2 #2 — feature/benefit (verb-noun shape)
8. H2 #3 — how-to ("Getting started")
9. H2 final — "Looking ahead"
10. Inline CTA + newsletter + related articles + footer
```

That's the chassis. Everything else (FAQ, code blocks, TL;DR, fact box, JSON-LD, disclaimer block, pull quote) is an Infinex-side addition layered on top of the Phantom skeleton.
