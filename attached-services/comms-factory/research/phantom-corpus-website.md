## Notable surface taxonomy observations

- /learn is split into FOUR sub-hubs, each with its own brand label: **Phantomblog** (news + product launches), **Crypto101** (educational explainers, beginner-tagged), **Getting started with Phantom** (product how-to guides), and **Developerresources** (dev docs). The compound-word styling ("Phantomblog", "Crypto101", "Developerresources") is consistent and load-bearing — Phantom treats sub-hubs as branded surfaces, not generic categories.
- Articles carry explicit difficulty tags: Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced, often paired with a chain or topic tag (Solana, Ethereum, Security, DeFi, Bridges). The /learn index does the audience-routing work the copy itself doesn't have to.
- The /get-started page mirrors the homepage's hero structure but reframes for first-touch: "money app that'll take you places" (homepage) becomes "crypto app for everyone" (get-started). Two-surface positioning: identity-forward vs. function-forward.
- Phantom has TWO distinct article voices: news/blog posts use first-person plural ("we're launching", "we've emerged", "thank you") with founder-style register; Crypto101 explainers use second-person tutorial register ("you'll always want to keep", "you're taking a gamble"). Useful for Laban — different surfaces likely sit in different placements.
