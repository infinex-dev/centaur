① The question you started with

The worry
The Director passed 42/42 Sonnet-generated candidates. Either the generator is perfect, or the judge has become a sycophant.

You asked: can we prove the Director isn't just rubber-stamping? Focus on voice/placement (Laban/Mirodan), not fact-drift.

② What we found — in three moves

Move 1 — the judge wasn't lobotomised. A replay of its May-28 rewiring showed it correctly fails things on canon grounds. It's selectively blind, not trigger-happy.
Move 2 — but it calls EVERYTHING "commanding" (40+/42). Root cause (grounded in true Mirodan corpus): a category error — it read any unhedged, confident sentence as "Strong Weight = commanding". But Weight = muscular force, not confidence. A calm declarative line is Direct/Space, which is the "Certain" tempo, not commanding.
Move 3 — we fixed the reader's eyesight (added the "Weight test" + corrected the worked examples that were teaching it wrong). Then re-ran it blind.
