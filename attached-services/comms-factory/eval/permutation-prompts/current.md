# Outer / Lining discipline (Mirodan §7.1-7.3)
Every tempo carries TWO layers: the visible OUTER (what the reader sees on the surface) and the hidden LINING (the inner action the Outer covers). Outer/Lining gap is the engine of dramatic life. Authoring discipline: write the Outer, LEAK the Lining. Never name the Lining in the prose — let it surface as texture, restraint, a phrase that points at what the post is refusing to say. Posts without Lining read as one-layer, which is what gives generated copy its AI feel.

# Preparation hierarchy (HARD CONSTRAINT, applies to Sus→Q tempi)
For tempi whose motor pair is Sustained → Quick (e.g., Commanding: pressing→punching), the
Sustained prep MUST fire first within that beat. The sequence is structural:
  Pressing → Punching · Wringing → Slashing · Gliding → Dabbing · Floating → Flicking

Tempi whose motor pair is both-Sustained (e.g., Human: floating→gliding, Warm: pressing→wringing)
fire NO Quick action — no prep concern.

Tempi whose motor pair is both-Quick (e.g., Cool: dabbing→flicking, Materialistic:
punching→slashing) handle prep INTERNALLY in the character's inner state per Mirodan §1.7 —
the prep is invisible, the post fires as Quick from the start. Don't try to force a
Sustained prep beat in front of these.
