# Drive mapping reference

Mirodan's four Drives: **Doing ┬À Spell ┬À Passion ┬À Vision.**

A Drive is the activated character energy that determines what vocabulary family is on-spec. Drives are derived **mechanically** from the placement ÔÇö once (Inner Attitude + Aspect + Stress) is locked, the Drive (and its Action Axis partner) falls out of the canonical 24-cell table below.

**Page references throughout are Mirodan vol 2** (`Mirodan-PhD-1997-Vol2.pdf`).

## How a Drive is derived (Mirodan ┬º3.1, pp. 525ÔÇô540)

A Drive is what fires when **3 of the 4 Motion Factors** are active. The Drive is named by the **latent factor** ÔÇö the one that ISN'T firing:

| Latent factor | Drive | Mirodan's quality pair (gloss) | Mental Factor (Carpenter) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Flow latent | **Doing** | exerting / reacting | Sensing |
| Time latent | **Spell** | dominating / surrendering | Intuiting |
| Space latent | **Passion** | constructing / destroying | Feeling |
| Weight latent | **Vision** | ideas / problems | Thinking |

A character's placement = Inner Attitude (2 factors) + Stress (1 factor) = **3 active factors**. The latent (4th) factor names the resting Drive.

> **Mirodan emendation (pp. 540ÔÇô541):** she proposed swapping SpellÔåöVision parent Mental Factors ÔÇö making Spell=Thinking, Vision=Intuiting. Non-standard. For comms-factory purposes use the Carpenter-canonical assignments above unless an operator explicitly opts into the emendation.

## The Action Axis ÔÇö why Spell-Vision and not just Spell

Each placement is an **X-diagram** with 4 corners (Mirodan pp. 551a, 553ÔÇô556):

```
TOP-LEFT                                 TOP-RIGHT
Main Inner Action Drive    -------    Main Outer Action Drive
(introvert ÔÇö hidden lining)            (extravert ÔÇö what shows)
        \                              /
         \      MAIN AXIS             /
          \   Spell ÔåÆ Vision         /
           \  (bottom-left to       /
            \  top-right)          /
             \                    /
BOTTOM-LEFT                                BOTTOM-RIGHT
Inner Character Drive       -------    Outer Character Drive
(dominant formative drive)              (secondary formative drive)
```

The **Main Character-Action Axis** (thick diagonal) runs bottom-left ÔåÆ top-right: from the resting **Inner Character Drive** to the projected **Main Outer Action Drive**. This is what we mean by "SpellÔåÆVision" or "DoingÔåÆPassion" ÔÇö the axis name.

The two **top** Drives are the two NOT in the formative pair. E.g. Adream is Passion/Spell formative ÔåÆ top corners are Doing + Vision. The Stress determines which sits at top-right (extravert) vs top-left (introvert).

## Why drives appear "absent" but aren't

A character spec'd as **Stable + Flow + Penetrating (Diagram D, SpellÔåÆVision)** still has Passion present at the top-left as the **introvert / hidden lining**. Passion is NOT absent ÔÇö it's the underlayer that never surfaces as the visible projection. What's off-spec is **Passion appearing as the extravert** (the visible main projection). Time-pressure / hype / FOMO copy is off-spec because it foregrounds Passion in the extravert slot, displacing Vision.

Soften any "no Passion at all" framing in voice docs ÔÇö the accurate statement is "no Passion as extravert."

## The 24 Action Diagrams ÔÇö canonical table (pp. 552ÔÇô557)

6 baselines ├ù 2 Aspects ├ù 2 Stresses = 24 cells. **Only Stable, Adream, Near are formative** ÔÇö the other 12 cells (Mobile, Remote, Awake) exist as Outer Action Attitudes inside the 12 embodied diagrams below, not as standalone characters (pp. 432, 474, 567).

### STABLE (Inner = Weight + Space; formative pair = Doing + Spell)

Stresses available: **Time** or **Flow** (never Space, never Weight). Aspects available: **Enclosing** (Weight-led) or **Penetrating** (Space-led). Diagram illustrations: p. 565a.

| Diagram | Stress | Aspect | Formative (BL/BR) | Main Axis (BLÔåÆTR) | Outer Actions | Canonical examples |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Time | Enclosing | Doing / Spell | **Doing ÔåÆ Passion** | Near + Awake | Hawthorne, Werle (one reading), Creon |
| **B** | Time | Penetrating | Doing / Spell | **Doing ÔåÆ Vision** | Near + Awake | Time-stressed intellectual rulers |
| **C** | Flow | Enclosing | Spell / Doing | **Spell ÔåÆ Passion** | Adream + Remote | Fortinbras (Adream outer) |
| **D** | Flow | Penetrating | Spell / Doing | **Spell ÔåÆ Vision** | Adream + Remote | **the Duke (Measure for Measure), Werle (Wild Duck) ÔÇö Infinex-locked** |

### ADREAM (Inner = Weight + Flow; formative pair = Passion + Spell)

Stresses available: **Time** or **Space** (never Flow, never Weight). Aspects available: **Enclosing** (Weight-led) or **Radiating** (Flow-led). Diagram illustrations: p. 561a.

| Diagram | Stress | Aspect | Formative (BL/BR) | Main Axis (BLÔåÆTR) | Outer Actions | Canonical examples |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Space | Enclosing | Spell / Passion | **Spell ÔåÆ Doing** | Near + Remote | Claudius, Brack, Stella |
| **B** | Space | Radiating | Spell / Passion | **Spell ÔåÆ Vision** | Stable + Mobile | Hamlet, Hedda Gabler, Miss Julie |
| **C** | Time | Enclosing | Passion / Spell | **Passion ÔåÆ Doing** | Near + Mobile | Gertrude, Stanley, Lear, Jean |
| **D** | Time | Radiating | Passion / Spell | **Passion ÔåÆ Vision** | Mobile + Stable | Ophelia, Lovborg, Blanche, Yakov |

### NEAR (Inner = Weight + Time; formative pair = Doing + Passion)

Stresses available: **Space** or **Flow** (never Time, never Weight). Aspects available: **Enclosing** (Weight-led) or **Circumscribing** (Time-led). Diagram illustrations: p. 563a.

| Diagram | Stress | Aspect | Formative (BL/BR) | Main Axis (BLÔåÆTR) | Outer Actions | Canonical examples |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Space | Circumscribing | Doing / Passion | **Doing ÔåÆ Spell** | Stable + Awake | Iago (if Near), Feste |
| **B** | Space | Enclosing | Doing / Passion | **Doing ÔåÆ Vision** | Stable + Awake | Maria |
| **C** | Flow | Circumscribing | Passion / Doing | **Passion ÔåÆ Spell** | Adream + Mobile | the Nurse (R&J) |
| **D** | Flow | Enclosing | Passion / Doing | **Passion ÔåÆ Vision** | Adream + Mobile | Sir Toby, Rebecca Nurse |

> **Mirodan typo (p. 564):** Rebecca Nurse is described as "(B)" but axis Passion-Vision belongs to Near-D. Cite the **axis** in any classifier, not the diagram letter.

## Drive-combo set ÔÇö 6 pairs, 3 embodied

Drives combine pairwise as "Confluence" (pp. 546ÔÇô548). 4-choose-2 = 6 possible pairs; only 3 form embodied characters:

| Combo | Forms Inner Attitude | Embodied? | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Doing + Spell | **Stable** | Ô£à yes | Werle, Logan Roy, the Duke |
| Doing + Passion | **Near** | Ô£à yes | Iago, the Nurse, Joe Rogan, Tony Soprano |
| Passion + Spell | **Adream** | Ô£à yes | Hamlet, Hedda, Stanley, Lear, Ophelia |
| Passion + Vision | Mobile | ÔØî outer state only | fires as Adream-D's or Near-D's top-right |
| Spell + Vision | Remote | ÔØî outer state only | fires as Stable-D's top-right |
| Doing + Vision | Awake | ÔØî outer state only | fires as Stable-B's or Near-A/B's top-right |

The 3 non-embodied combos surface only inside the 12 embodied diagrams above, as the Main Outer Action Drive (top-right of the X).

## Stress as status grammar (Mirodan ┬º4.4, p. 458)

> "The more developed (more mature or higher in status) a character is, the more likely it is that it will have a Space-stress. Less developed Near characters are Flow-stressed, while Adream characters which are lower either in intelligence or in social position are Time-stressed."

| Stress | Reads as |
|---|---|
| **Time** | adolescent / lower status / immature / market-pulse / urgency |
| **Space** | mature / higher status / sophisticated / strategic |
| **Flow** | emotional / sociable / yielding |
| ~~Weight~~ | **Weight is NEVER a stress** ÔÇö it's always in the baseline's inner pair |

**For brand voice:** Time-stress reads adolescent. If a brand wants to read mature/grown-up, Space or Flow.

## Drive ÔåÆ vocabulary footprint (operational)

Each Drive has a vocabulary family. Lock the placement ÔåÆ identify the formative + axis drives ÔåÆ reject anything that activates an OFF-spec drive.

### Doing-on-spec phrases
- "Shipped." "Built." "Live." "Now in production."
- Past-perfect + present-tense.
- Concrete completed actions.

### Spell-on-spec phrases
- "We've been building this for years."
- "Section by section."
- "Patient."
- "The craft of [X]."
- "Has always been about [Y]."
- Long-arc, timeless framing.

### Passion-on-spec phrases (off-spec for Spell-only or Vision-dominant characters)
- "Act now." "Today only." "Don't miss." "Last chance." "Hurry."
- "Buckle up." "Let's go." "Wagmi." "FOMO."
- "Right now / time's running out / get in while you can."
- Anything that activates urgency-as-virtue.

### Vision-on-spec phrases
- "A few months from now..." "In a couple of years..."
- "What this lets your agent do..."
- "The world this opens..."
- "Stop having to [current pain] ÔÇö soon."
- Future-tense as the dominant grammatical move.

## Off-spec regex example ÔÇö Infinex (Stable-D, SpellÔåÆVision)

Passion is the introvert lining (top-left of Diagram D), Vision the extravert. Passion-vocabulary surfacing in the extravert slot displaces Vision and reads off-spec.

```typescript

    reason: "time-pressure activates Passion as extravert ÔÇö off-spec for SpellÔåÆVision",
```

## How to derive Drive from a placement (decision recipe)

1. Confirm the placement is legal (use the available-stresses + available-aspects rule per Inner Attitude).
2. Find the row in the 24-cell table above.
3. The **formative pair** is bottom-left + bottom-right of the X (the Inner Character Drive + Outer Character Drive).
4. The **Main Axis** is bottom-left ÔåÆ top-right (resting Drive ÔåÆ projected Outer Action Drive).
5. The **introvert / hidden lining** is the top-left.
6. Off-spec drives = the two NOT named in the formative pair + main axis. For Stable-D (formative Spell/Doing, axis SpellÔåÆVision, lining Passion): only Spell, Doing, Vision are on-spec. Passion is on-spec only as introvert lining, never as extravert.
