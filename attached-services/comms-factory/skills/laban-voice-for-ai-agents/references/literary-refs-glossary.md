# Literary references — modern equivalents

Mirodan's framework uses canonical theatrical characters as placement exemplars. Operators unfamiliar with Williams / Ibsen / Shakespeare / Sophocles can't evaluate the archetypes. This file glosses each ref with a one-line description + modern equivalents that hit closer.

Cross-link: when other files in this skill cite a literary ref (e.g., "Werle / Duke shape"), readers can come here for the gloss.

## How to use

When designing a voice spec, you don't need to know the original characters. Use the modern equivalents as the operator-facing image. Mirodan's characters are framework anchors, not required cultural knowledge.

## Stable baseline (intelligent ruler, decisions taken)

### Werle (Ibsen, *The Wild Duck*, 1884)
**Placement:** Stable + Flow-stressed + Penetrating (the softer-yielding Stable, Diagram D, Spell→Vision axis; Passion is hidden lining, not visible projection)
**One-line:** The wise old guard who has long settled his decisions; under pressure, feeling visibly yields but the inner gravity holds.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Logan Roy** (Succession, late episodes) — settled, declining, the feeling cracks through the institutional cover
- **Don Corleone** (early Godfather scenes) — patriarch whose conviction is decades-old, who can be moved
- **Stephen Hawking voice** in late interviews — patience + intellectual gravity + visible warmth

### The Duke (Shakespeare, *Measure for Measure*)
**Placement:** Stable + Flow-stressed + Penetrating (same family as Werle)
**One-line:** The ruler who watches from behind, intervenes from a position of moral gravity, doesn't need credit.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Dumbledore** (Harry Potter) — wise overseer, lets others move, knows the longer arc
- **Aragorn** (LOTR, post-coronation) — ruler whose decisions are taken, holds gravitas without spectacle

### Creon (Sophocles, *Antigone*, *Oedipus Rex*)
**Placement:** Stable + Time-stressed + Enclosing (the brutal-rock Stable, Diagram A, Doing-Passion)
**One-line:** Hard institutional ruler whose decisions are absolute; brutal when crossed.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Walter White** (Breaking Bad, mid-series) — decisive, unyielding, willing to hurt
- **Tywin Lannister** (Game of Thrones) — institutional gravity, cold position
- **Gregg Popovich** stoic mode — coach who has decided, won't be moved

### Hawthorne (Updike's *Roger's Version* / various)
**Placement:** Stable + Time-stressed + Enclosing
**One-line:** Sensuous, weighty, decided. The body-grounded ruler.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Tony Soprano** (early seasons, in his prime) — body-forward, decided, will use force
- **Hank Schrader** (Breaking Bad) — physical authority

## Adream baseline (vision-pulled, the lover, doubly-unaware)

### Hamlet (Shakespeare)
**Placement:** Adream + Space-stressed + Radiating (Diagram B, Spell-Vision but mixes in Passion)
**One-line:** Vision-pulled-but-torn intellectual whose feeling exceeds his grounding; the "to be or not to be" mind that can't stop interrogating itself.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Don Draper** (Mad Men) — vision-pulled, philosophically tortured, can't quite land
- **Hannah Horvath** (Girls) — feeling exceeds grounding, vision-of-self never matches lived self
- **Adam Curtis voice** in his documentaries — vision-pulled, big-picture-pulled

### Hedda Gabler (Ibsen, *Hedda Gabler*)
**Placement:** Adream + Space-stressed + Radiating (Diagram B)
**One-line:** Aristocratic-aloof vision-pulled, contained but feeling radiates; bored, intelligent, dangerous.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Daisy Buchanan** (Gatsby) — aristocratic, feeling radiates through the mask
- **Killing Eve's Villanelle** — contained feeling that radiates outward unpredictably

### Claudius (Shakespeare, *Hamlet*)
**Placement:** Adream + Space-stressed + Enclosing (Diagram A, Spell-Doing)
**One-line:** Sensuous, contained, ruling from a position the audience knows is corrupt.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Frank Underwood** (House of Cards) — sensuous power, settled in
- **Logan Roy** (Succession) — Stable variant, but the sensual-contained register is close

### Othello (Shakespeare)
**Placement:** Adream + Time-stressed → Enclosing variant
**One-line:** The lover-warrior whose feeling explodes when challenged; vision of self breaks under pressure.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Rust Cohle** (True Detective S1) — vision-pulled, feeling-radiates-when-cracked
- **Frank Castle / Punisher** — warrior whose grief is the engine

### Ophelia (Shakespeare, *Hamlet*) · Lovborg (Ibsen, *Hedda Gabler*) · Blanche DuBois (Williams, *A Streetcar Named Desire*) · Miss Julie (Strindberg)
**Placement:** Adream + Time-stressed + Radiating (Diagram D, Passion-Vision)
**One-line:** Vision-pulled-and-broken; feeling pours out when grounding gives way. The doomed-lover archetype.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Hannah Horvath** (Girls, breakdown episodes) — feeling pours out, vision shattered
- **Marianne Dashwood** (Sense and Sensibility) — feeling-radiating, vision-broken
- **Cassie** (Euphoria) — vision-of-self pours out, broken-romantic

## Near baseline (down-to-earth, sensory, relating)

### Big Daddy (Williams, *Cat on a Hot Tin Roof*, 1955)
**Placement:** Near + Flow-stressed + Enclosing
**One-line:** Body-talking, sensory-blunt, dying patriarch. "I want bourbon and I want it now." Body IS the discourse.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Tony Soprano** (early seasons) — body talking, visceral, sensory-immediate
- **Anthony Bourdain voice** — "this fish tastes like Mediterranean evening sweat"
- **Sam Elliott characters** — weight-in-the-vowels, body-forward
- **Ron Swanson** — meat/wood/whiskey-as-philosophy
- **Joe Rogan persona** — body-forward, visceral, present-tense

### Tesman (Ibsen, *Hedda Gabler*)
**Placement:** Near + Space-stressed + Circumscribing
**One-line:** Well-mannered pedant; uses inherited academic form as voice mediator. Polite, conventional, slightly oblivious.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Niles Crane / Frasier Crane** (Frasier) — inherited form, polite-academic register
- **Wes Anderson narrators** — Royal Tenenbaums voice, Steve Zissou, M. Gustave
- **The Dowager Countess** (Downton Abbey) — inherited form as personal weapon
- **Stephen Fry persona** — Edwardian/literary form as identity

### Stanley Kowalski (Williams, *A Streetcar Named Desire*)
**Placement:** Near + Time-stressed + Enclosing (Adream Time-stressed Enclosing per Mirodan §3.2, Diagram C — Passion-Doing)
**One-line:** Pure body, raw sensory immediate; feeling and force together.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Walter White** (Breaking Bad, mid-series) — present-tense fury, body-language as voice
- **Tony Soprano** rage episodes
- **Marlon Brando** in any of his iconic roles

### Lear (Shakespeare, *King Lear*)
**Placement:** Near + Flow-stressed → multiple Adream-outer projections (the "split" mechanic per Mirodan §3.2). Actually a complex case — Lear deteriorates through the play; Mirodan tracks the shift.
**One-line:** Aged patriarch whose grounding fails; feeling overwhelms; from ruler to broken vision.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Logan Roy** (late Succession) — patriarch in decline
- **Don Corleone** (Godfather Part II, Vito's final scenes)

### Gertrude (Shakespeare, *Hamlet*)
**Placement:** Adream + Time-stressed + Enclosing (Diagram C)
**One-line:** Sensuous, conflicted, body-forward, knows-but-won't-name. The complicit queen.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Cersei Lannister** (early Game of Thrones)

### Jean (Strindberg, *Miss Julie*)
**Placement:** Adream + Time-stressed + Enclosing (Diagram C)
**One-line:** Servant-ambitious; feeling fires opportunistically when status moves are available.
**Modern equivalents:**
- **Tom Wambsgans** (Succession) — opportunistic, status-climbing, feeling visible when ambition fires

## Outer Action Attitudes (never baselines; fire as projections only)

These have no baseline character anchors because they're STATES, not baselines. When you see them in tempi names (Sociable, Egocentric, Acute, etc), they refer to outer projections of one of the three baselines under stress.

- **Mobile** (Quick + Flow axis) — fires for Adream-Time-stressed or Near-Flow-stressed characters
- **Remote** (Space + Flow axis) — fires for Adream-Space-stressed or Stable-Flow-stressed characters
- **Awake** (Space + Time axis) — fires for Stable-Time-stressed or Near-Space-stressed characters

Modern parallels for the outer states (qualitative, not character-anchored):
- Mobile = fragmentation under pressure (Hannah Horvath breakdown mode, anyone splitting)
- Remote = cordial-distance / aristocratic-aloof (any "old-money detached" persona)
- Awake = intellectual sensor (any "investigator/strategist" register — Nick Fury, Sherlock)

## How to apply when running the skill

When you encounter a Mirodan ref in another file:
1. Check this glossary for the modern equivalent
2. Use the modern equivalent as the operator-facing image
3. The Mirodan name only matters for framework lookup (drive sub-axis, outer projection map)

When generating samples for a brand: pick 2-3 modern equivalents from the SAME PLACEMENT family and tell the model "the character sits between Tony Soprano and Wes Anderson narrator energy" — concrete, evocative, no theatrical-history required.

## Caveats

- Modern equivalents are approximate. A character is never a perfect 1:1 — they're anchors for the felt-signature.
- Different operators may resonate with different equivalents. Pick what works for YOUR operator's cultural fluency.
- Mirodan's originals carry framework-specific information that modern equivalents may not preserve fully. When in doubt, fall back to the framework mechanics (motor signatures, drive lock, outer projection map).
